====== 8. God Saves Joseph and His Family ======
====== Iniligtas ng Diyos si Jose at ang Kanyang Pamilya ======

{{:en:obs:obs-08-02.jpg?nolink&640x360}}

Many years later, when Jacob was an old man, he sent his favorite son, Joseph, to check on his older brothers who were far away, taking care of the herds.

Noong matanda na si Jacob, inutusan niya ang paborito niyang anak na si Jose para tingnan kung ano ang ginagawa ng iba niyang mga anak na lalaki. Nasa malayo kasi ang mga ito at ipinapastol ang mga hayop na alaga nila.

{{:en:obs:obs-08-01.jpg?nolink&640x360}}

Joseph’s brothers hated him because their father loved him most and because Joseph had dreamed that he would be their ruler. When Joseph found his brothers, they kidnapped him and sold him to some slave traders.

Galit kay Jose ang mga kapatid niya dahil paborito ng tatay nila si Jose. Saka nanaginip pa si Jose na siya daw ay mamumuno sa kanilang magkakapatid. Nang magkita na sila, hinawakan nila si Jose at ipinagbili sa mga bumibili ng alipin.

{{:en:obs:obs-08-03.jpg?nolink&640x360}}

Before Joseph's brothers returned home, they tore Joseph’s robe and dipped it in goat's blood. Then they showed the robe to their father so that he would think a wild animal had killed Joseph. Jacob was very sad.

Bago sila umuwi, kinuha nila ang damit ni Jose, sinira ito at inilubog sa dugo ng kambing. Ipinakita nila ito sa kanilang ama. Akala tuloy ni Jacob ay napatay ng mababangis na hayop si Jose. Malungkot na malungkot si Jacob.

{{:en:obs:obs-08-04.jpg?nolink&640x360}}

The slave traders took Joseph to Egypt. Egypt was a large, powerful country located along the Nile River. The slave traders sold Joseph as a slave to a wealthy government official. Joseph served his master well, and God blessed Joseph.

Samantala, sa Egipto dinala si Jose ng mga nakabili sa kanya. Malaki at makapangyarihang bansa ang Egipto. Makikita ito sa tabi ng Ilog Nilo. Ibinenta nila si Jose sa isang mayamang tauhan ng gobyerno. Maging masunurin si Jose sa kanyang amo, kaya naman pinagpala siya ng Diyos.

{{:en:obs:obs-08-05.jpg?nolink&640x360}}

His master’s wife tried to sleep with Joseph, but Joseph refused to sin against God in this way. She became angry and falsely accused Joseph so that he was arrested and sent to prison. Even in prison, Joseph remained faithful to God, and God blessed him.

Pero inaakit ng asawa ng amo niya si Jose. Hindi pumayag si Jose dahil kasalanan iyon sa Diyos. Kaya nagalit sa kanya ang asawa ng amo niya. PInagbintangan niya si Jose ng hindi totoo. Dahil dito, hinuli si Jose at ikinulong. Pero kahit na nasa kulungan si Jose, palagi pa din siyang nagtitiwala sa Diyos, kaya pinagpala siya ng Diyos.

{{:en:obs:obs-08-06.jpg?nolink&640x360}}

After two years, Joseph was still in prison, even though he was innocent. One night, the king of Egypt, who was called the Pharaoh, had two dreams that disturbed him greatly. None of his advisors could tell him the meaning of the dreams.

Pagkalipas ng dalawang taon, nakakulong pa din si Jose kahit wala siyang kasalanan. Isang gabi, nanaginip ang hari ng Egipto. Dalawang beses nanaginip ang hari at masyado siyang nag-alala. Wala namang makapagpaliwanag sa panaginip niya.

{{:en:obs:obs-08-07.jpg?nolink&640x360}}

God had given Joseph the ability to interpret dreams, so Pharaoh had Joseph brought to him from the prison. Joseph interpreted the dreams for him and said, “God is going to send seven years of good harvests followed by seven years of famine.”

Kaya ipinatawag ng hari si Jose, dahil nabalitaan niya na si Jose ay binigyan ng Diyos ng kakayahang magpaliwanag ng panaginip. Ipinaliwanag ni Jose ang panaginip ng hari. Sabi niya, “Magpapadala po ang Diyos ng pitong taon ng masaganag ani, Pero pagkatapos po ay pitong taon naman ng taggutom  at walang ani.”

{{:en:obs:obs-08-08.jpg?nolink&640x360}}

Pharaoh was so impressed with Joseph that he made him the second most powerful man in all of Egypt!

Humanga ang hari kay Jose kaya ginawa niya itong tagapamahala sa buong Egipto. Siya ang sumunod na pinakamataas sa hari ng Egipto.

{{:en:obs:obs-08-09.jpg?nolink&640x360}}

Joseph told the people to store up large amounts of food during the seven years of good harvests. Then Joseph sold the food to the people when the seven years of famine came so they would have enough to eat.

Inutusan ni Jose ang mga taga-Egipto, “Mag-iipon tayo ng pagkain sa loob ng pitong taon na sagana ang ani para mayroon tayong makakain kapag dumating na ang pitong taon na walang maaani.”

{{:en:obs:obs-08-10.jpg?nolink&640x360}}

The famine was severe not only in Egypt, but also in Canaan where Jacob and his family lived.

Hindi lang sa Egipto nagkaroon ng taggutom. Naranasan din ito ng pamilya ni Jacob na nakatira sa Canaan.

{{:en:obs:obs-08-11.jpg?nolink&640x360}}

So Jacob sent his sons to Egypt to buy food. The brothers did not recognize Joseph when they stood before him to buy food. But Joseph recognized them.

Kaya inutusan ni Jacob ang mga anak niyang lalaki para bumili ng pagkain sa Egipto.  Hindi nila nakilala si Jose kahit na nasa harap nila ito habang bumibili sila ng pagkain. Pero nakilala sila ni Jose.

{{:en:obs:obs-08-12.jpg?nolink&640x360}}

After testing his brothers to see if they had changed, Joseph said to them, “I am your brother, Joseph! Do not be afraid. You tried to do evil when you sold me as a slave, but God used the evil for good! Come and live in Egypt so I can provide for you and your families.”

Nagpakilala lang sa kanila si Jose nang mapatunayan niyang nagbago na ang ugali ng mga kapatid niya. Sinabi ni Jose sa kanila, “Ako ang kapatid ninyong si Jose! Huwag kayong matakot. Kahit masama ang plano ninyo sa akin nung ipinagbili ninyo ako para maging alipin, pinaganda naman ng Diyos ang nangyari sa akin! Dito na kayo tumira kasama ko sa Egipto para mabigyan ko kayo ng pagkain pati ang pamilya ninyo.”

{{:en:obs:obs-08-13.jpg?nolink&640x360}}

When Joseph's brothers returned home and told their father, Jacob, that Joseph was still alive, he was very happy.

Pag-uwi ng mga kapatid ni Jose, ikinuwento nila kay Jacob na buhay pa si Jose. Masayang masaya si Jacob nang malaman ito.

{{:en:obs:obs-08-14.jpg?nolink&640x360}}

Even though Jacob was an old man, he moved to Egypt with all of his family, and they all lived there. Before Jacob died, he blessed each of his sons.

Kaya kahit matanda na si Jacob, pumunta pa rin siya sa Egipto kasama ng pamilya niya. Doon na sila tumirang lahat. At bago siya mamatay, pinagpala muna niya ang lahat ng mga anak niya.

{{:en:obs:obs-08-15.jpg?nolink&640x360}}

The covenant promises that God gave to Abraham were passed on to Isaac, then to Jacob, and then to Jacob’s twelve sons and their families. The descendants of the twelve sons became the twelve tribes of Israel.

Ang pangako ng Diyos kay Abraham ay ipinapatuloy niya kay Isaac, at pagkatapos ay kay Jacob at pagkatapos ay sa labindalawang anak ni Jacob at sa kani-kanilang pamilya. Ang lahi ng mga anak niya ang naging labindalawang tribo ng Israel.

//A Bible story from: Genesis 37-50//
