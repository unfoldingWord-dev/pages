====== 14. Wandering in the Wilderness ======
====== Naglakbay ng mga Israelita sa Ilang =======

{{:en:obs:obs-14-01.jpg?nolink&640x360}}

After the Israelites received God’s Covenant at Mount Sinai, God began leading them toward the Promised Land, which was also called Canaan. The cloud pillar showed them the way.

Nang matanggap na ng mga Israelita ang utos ng Diyos, pinangunahan sila ng Diyos sa paglalakbay papunta sa lupaing ipinangako sa kanila ng Diyos. Ang haliging ulap ang nanguna sa kanila.

{{:en:obs:obs-14-02.jpg?nolink&640x360}}

God had promised Abraham, Isaac, and Jacob that he would give the Promised Land to their descendants, but now there were many people groups living there. They were called Canaanites. The Canaanites did not believe in God. They worshiped false gods and did many evil things.

Ipinangako ng Diyos na ibibigay niya kina Abraham, Isaac at Jacob at sa kanilang mga anak ang lupain ng Canaan. Kaya lang, maraming nakatira sa lupaing iyon. Ang tawag sa kanila ay mga taga-Canaan. Hindi naniniwala sa Diyos ang mga taga-Canaan. Ang sinasamba nila ay ang mga diyus-diyusan at ibang mga bagay.

{{:en:obs:obs-14-03.jpg?nolink&640x360}}

God told the Israelites, “You must get rid of all the Canaanites in the Promised Land. Do not make peace with them and do not marry them. You must completely destroy all of their idols. If you do not obey me, you will worship their idols instead of me.”

Sinabi ng Diyos sa mga Israelita, “Kailangang mawala ang mga taga-Canaan sa lupaing iyon. Huwag kayong makipagkaibigan sa kanila. Huwag din kayong magpapakasal sa kanila. Kailangang sirain ninyo ang kanilang mga rebulto. Sundin ninyo ako at huwag ninyong sasambahin ang mga rebulto nila.

{{:en:obs:obs-14-04.jpg?nolink&640x360}}

When the Israelites reached the edge of Canaan, Moses chose twelve men, one from each tribe of Israel. He gave the men instructions to go and spy on the land to see what it was like. They were also to spy on the Canaanites to see if they were strong or weak.

Nang malapit na ang mga Israelita sa Canaan, pumili si Moises ng 12 lalaki. Nagmula sila sa 12 tribo ng Israel. Inutusan sila ni Moises na tingnan ang lupain. Titingnan din nila kung malalakas o mahihina ang mga nakatira doon.

{{:en:obs:obs-14-05.jpg?nolink&640x360}}

The twelve men spied on Canaan for forty days and then they came back. Ten of the spies gave a bad report, saying, “The land is very good, and the crops are great! But the cities are very strong, and the people are giants! If we attack them, we will surely die!”

Apatnapung araw nanatili doon ang mga inutusan ni Moises. Pagbalik nila, sinabi ng sampu, “Maganda ang lupain; malulusog ang mga halaman! Pero matibay ang mga lungsod doon. Higante ang nakatira! Mamamatay lang tayo kapag nilabanan natin sila!”

{{:en:obs:obs-14-06.jpg?nolink&640x360}}

Immediately Caleb and Joshua, the other two spies, said, “It is true that the people of Canaan are tall and strong, but we can certainly defeat them! God will fight for us!”

Pero sinabi nina Caleb at Josue, “Totoo na malalaki at malalakas ang mga nakatira doon, pero kaya natin silang talunin. Ang Diyos ang lalaban sa kanila para sa atin.”

{{:en:obs:obs-14-07.jpg?nolink&640x360}}

But the people were mad at Moses and Aaron and said, “Oh, why did you bring us to this horrible place? We should have stayed in Egypt rather than be killed in battle and our wives and children made slaves.” The people wanted to kill them and choose a new leader to bring them back to Egypt.

Nagalit ang mga tao kina Moises at Aaron. Sabi nila, “Bakit ninyo kami dinala dito? Dapat sana ay doon na lang kami sa Ehipto. Mamamatay lang pala kami dito, peti ang mga asawa namin at mga anak.” Gusto na nilang patayin sina Moises at Aaron at pumili ng bagong pinuno para makabalik sila sa Ehipto.

{{:en:obs:obs-14-08.jpg?nolink&640x360}}

God was very angry, and his presence appeared at the Tent of Meeting. God said, “Except for Joshua and Caleb, all of the people who are twenty years old or older will wander in the wilderness until they die. They will never enter the Promised Land.”

Dahil dito, nagalit sa kanila ang Diyos. Sinabi ng Diyos, “Sina Caleb at Josue lang ang makakapasok sa lupaing ipinangako ko sa inyo. Lahat ng may edad na 20 pataas ay maglalakbay sa ilang hanggang sila ay mamatay. Hindi sila makakapasok sa lupaing ipinangako ko sa inyo.

{{:en:obs:obs-14-09.jpg?nolink&640x360}}

When the people heard this, they were sorry they had sinned. They took their weapons and went to attack Canaan. Moses warned them not to go because God was not with them, but they did not listen to him.

Nang marinig ito ng mga tao, nalungkot sila dahil mali ang ginawa nila. Kinuha nila ang mga sandata nila at pumunta sa Canaan para lumaban. Sinabi sa kanila ni Moises, “Huwag! Hindio kayo tutulungan ng Diyos.” Pero hindi sila nakinig kay Moises.

{{:en:obs:obs-14-10.jpg?nolink&640x360}}

God did not go with them into this battle, so they were defeated and many of them were killed. Then the Israelites turned back from Canaan and wandered through the wilderness for forty years.

Natalo sila, dahil hindi sila tinulungan ng Diyos. Kaya bumalik sila at naglakbay sa ilang sa loob ng apatnapung taon.

{{:en:obs:obs-14-11.jpg?nolink&640x360}}

During that time of wandering, God provided for them. He gave them bread from heaven, called “manna.” He also sent quail (which is a kind of bird) into their camp so they could have meat to eat. During those forty years, God kept their clothes and shoes from wearing out.

Ibinigay ng Diyos ang mga kailangan nila sa kanilang paglalakbay. Binigyan sila ng Diyos ng tinapay na mula sa langit. Manna ang itnawag nila sa tinapay. Binigyan din sila ng Diyos ng pugo para makakain sila ng karne. Sa loob ng 40 taon, hindi napunit ang mga damit nila at hindi nasira ang mga panyapak nila.

{{:en:obs:obs-14-12.jpg?nolink&640x360}}

God even miraculously gave them water from a rock. But despite all this, the people of Israel complained and grumbled against God and against Moses. God was still faithful to his promises to Abraham, Isaac, and Jacob.

Nagpalabas din ng tubig mula sa bato ang Diyos. Pero sa kabila ng lahat, nagrereklamo pa rin sa Diyos at kay Moises ang mga tao. Pero nanatiling tapat ang Diyos sa mga pangako niya kina Abraham, Isaac, at Jacob.

{{:en:obs:obs-14-13.jpg?nolink&640x360}}

One time, the people had no water. God told Moses, “Speak to the rock, and water will come out of it.” But Moses dishonored God in front of all the people by hitting the rock twice with a stick instead of speaking to it. Water came out of the rock for everyone to drink, but God was angry with Moses and said, “You will not enter the Promised Land.”

Isang araw, walang mainom ang mga tao. SInabi ng Diyos kay Moises, “Kausapin mo ang bato at may lalabs na tubig mula doon.” Pero hindi sinunod ni Moises ang Diyos. Dalawang beses niyang hinampas ng tungkod ang bato sa halip na kausapin ito. May lumabas na tubig mula sa bato, pero nagalit ang Diyos sa ginawa ni Moises. Sinabi ng Diyos, “Hindi ka makakapasok sa lupaing ipinangako ko.”

{{:en:obs:obs-14-14.jpg?nolink&640x360}}

After the Israelites had wandered in the wilderness for forty years, all the people who had rebelled against God were dead. Then God led the people to the edge of the Promised Land again. Moses was now very old, so he chose Joshua to help him lead the people. God promised Moses that one day he would raise up another prophet like Moses.

Pagkalipas ng 40 taon, lahat ng mga taong hindi nagtiwala sa Diyos ay namatay na. Nakarating na rin sila sa malapit sa lupaing ipinangako ng Diyos. Matanda na si Moises kaya pinili niya si Josue para siya ang manguna sa mga tao. Ipinangako ng Diyos na baling araw, may isang propetang katulad ni Moises na mabubuhay sa mundo.

{{:en:obs:obs-14-15.jpg?nolink&640x360}}

God brought Moses to the top of a mountain so he could see the Promised Land. Moses saw the Promised Land but never entered it. Then Moses died, and the Israelites mourned for thirty days. Joshua became their new leader. Joshua was a good leader because he trusted and obeyed God.

Pinaakyat ng Diyos si Moises sa isang mataas na bundok para makita niya ang lupaing ibibigay sa kanila ng Diyos. Natanaw ni Moises ang lupain pero hindi siya nakarating doon. Namatay si Moises sa bundok at nagluksa ang mga tao sa loob ng 30 araw. Pagkalipas ng pagluluksa, si Josue na ang nanguna sa kanila. Naging mabuting pinuno si Josue dahil nagtitiwala at sumusunod siya sa Diyos.
