====== Messiah ======


==== (Messiah, Holy One, Christ) ====


===== Facts: =====

"Messiah" means, “Anointed One” or, “Chosen One” and refers to God's Son, whom God the Father appointed to save people from sin and death. 

  * Hundreds of years before the Messiah came, God's prophets told many things about the Messiah. These prophecies are written in the Bible.
  * The Messiah is a descendent of King David.
  * Jesus did many miraculous works to prove that he is the Messiah.
  * During Jesus' time living on earth, he fulfilled many of those prophecies; someday he will come back and fulfill the rest of the prophecies about the Messiah.
  * In the Greek language, the word for Messiah is “Christ.”

[See also: **[[:vi:key-terms:god-son|Son of God]]**, **David**, **[[:vi:key-terms:jesus|Jesus]]**]


===== Examples from the Bible stories: =====


  ***[17-07]** The **Messiah** is God's Chosen One who would save the world from sin. 
  ***[17-08]** The Israelites would have to wait a long time before the **Messiah** came, almost 1,000 years. 
  ***[21-01]** From the very beginning, God had a plan to send the **Messiah**. 
  ***[21-04]** God promised David that the **Messiah** would be one of David's own descendants. 
  ***[21-05]** The **Messiah** would start the New Covenant. 
  ***[21-06]** God's prophets also said that the coming **Messiah** would be a prophet, a priest, and a king. 
  ***[21-09]** Isaiah prophesied that the **Messiah** would be born from a virgin. 
  ***[43-07]** “But God raised him to life again to fulfill the prophecy which says, ‘You will not let your **Holy One** rot in the grave.’”
  ***[43-09]** "Know for certain that God has made this Jesus, the one you crucified, both Lord and **Messiah**!"
  ***[43-11]** Peter said, “Repent and be baptized every one of you in the name of Jesus **Christ** for the forgiveness of sins, and you will receive the gift of the Holy Spirit.”
  ***[46-06]** Saul argued with the Jews, proving that Jesus was the **Messiah**.
