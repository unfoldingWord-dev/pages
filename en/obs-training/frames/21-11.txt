====== Messiah Will Be Betrayed [21-11] ======

{{:en:obs:obs-21-11.jpg?nolink&640x360}}

**[[:en:key-terms:isaiah|Isaiah]]** **[[:en:key-terms:prophet|prophesied]]** that the **[[:en:key-terms:messiah|Messiah]]** would be **hated without reason** and rejected. Other **[[:en:key-terms:prophet|prophets]]** foretold that those who killed the Messiah would **gamble for his clothes** and he would be **[[:en:key-terms:betray|betrayed]]** by a friend. **Zechariah** foretold that the friend would be paid **thirty pieces of silver** for betraying him.


===== Translation Notes: =====


  * **hated without reason** - That is, "hated although he did nothing wrong" or "hated although he was innocent."
  * **gamble for his clothes** - They would use a game of chance to determine who would win his clothes.
  * **Zechariah** - Zechariah was an Old Testament prophet who spoke to Judah after they returned from the Exile in Babylon.
  * **thirty pieces of silver** - Each of these thirty silver coins were worth approximately four days worth of wages.
