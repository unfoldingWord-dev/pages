====== Messiah Will Be Betrayed [21-11] ======

{{:en:obs:obs-21-11.jpg?nolink&640x360}}

The prophet **[[:en:key-terms:isaiah|Isaiah]]** **[[:en:key-terms:prophet|prophesied]]** that the **[[:en:key-terms:messiah|Messiah]]** would be **hated without reason and rejected**. Other **[[:en:key-terms:prophet|prophets]]** foretold that those who killed the Messiah would **gamble for his clothes** and he would be **[[:en:key-terms:betray|betrayed]]** by a friend. The prophet **Zechariah foretold** that the friend would be paid **thirty pieces of silver for betraying him**.


===== Translation Notes: =====


  * **hated without reason and rejected** - This could be translated as, “hated and rejected, even though he had done nothing wrong” or “…even though he was innocent.” 
  * **gamble for his clothes** - That is, “play a game of chance to determine who would win his clothes.” 
  * **Zechariah** - Zechariah was an Old Testament prophet who spoke to Judah after they returned from the Exile in Babylon.
  * **foretold** – This means he told about things that would happen in the future. Other words that have a similar meaning are, “predicted” and “prophesied.” 
  * **thirty pieces of silver** - That is, “thirty silver coins.” At the time, each of these coins was worth the amount of money a person could earn in four days.   
  * **for betraying him** – That is, “in exchange for betraying him” or, “as payment for betraying him.”
