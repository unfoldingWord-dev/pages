====== The Great High Priest [48-06] ======

{{:en:obs:obs-48-06.jpg?nolink&640x360}}

**[[:en:key-terms:melchizedek |Melchizedek ]]** was a **[[:en:key-terms:priest|priest]]** who **[[:en:key-terms:bless|blessed]]** **[[:en:key-terms:abraham|Abraham]]**. Like Melchizedek, **[[:en:key-terms:jesus|Jesus]]** is a priest, but Jesus is the **[[:en:key-terms:priest-high|Great High Priest]]**. Jesus **offered only one [[:en:key-terms:sacrifice|sacrifice]], himself**. **He was the only sacrifice needed** to take away the **[[:en:key-terms:sin|sin]]** **of the whole world**. Jesus took the **[[:en:key-terms:punish|punishment]]** for every sin that anyone has ever committed.

**[[:en:key-terms:melchizedek |Melchizedek ]]** was a **[[:en:key-terms:priest|priest]]** who **[[:en:key-terms:bless|blessed]]** **[[:en:key-terms:abraham|Abraham]]**. Priests continually offered **[[:en:key-terms:sacrifice|sacrifices]]** as payment for people's **[[:en:key-terms:sin|sin]]** because those sacrifices could not take away sin. **[[:en:key-terms:jesus|Jesus]]** is the **[[:en:key-terms:priest-high|Great High Priest]]**. Unlike other priests, **he offered only one sacrifice, himself**. He was the only sacrifice that could to take away the sin **of the whole world**. Jesus was the perfect high priest because he **took the [[:en:key-terms:punish|punishment]]** for every sin that anyone has ever committed.


===== Translation Notes: =====

  * **he offered only one sacrifice, himself** - This could also be translated as, “Jesus made only one sacrifice. Instead of sacrificing an animal, he allowed himself to be killed as the sacrifice.”
  * **took the punishment** - That is, "received the punishment in his own body."
  * **of the whole world** - That is, “of all the people in the world.”
