====== Two Trees [01-11] ======


{{:en:obs:obs-01-11.jpg?nolink&640x360}}


**In the middle** of the **garden**, **[[:en:key-terms:god|God]]** planted two special trees—the **tree of [[:en:key-terms:life|life]]** and the **tree of the knowledge** of **[[:en:key-terms:good|good]]** and **[[:en:key-terms:evil|evil]]**. **[[:en:key-terms:god|God]]**  told **[[:en:key-terms:Adam|Adam]]**  that he could eat from any tree in the garden except from the tree of the knowledge of **[[:en:key-terms:good|good]]** and **[[:en:key-terms:evil|evil]]**. If he ate from this tree, he would **[[:en:key-terms:death|die]]**.


===== Translation Notes: =====


  * **in the middle** - The central location emphasizes the importance of the two trees.
  * **garden** -  An area of land planted with trees and plants for a purpose–usually to produce food or to provide beauty. 
  * **tree of [[:en:key-terms:life|life]]** - Anyone who ate fruit from this tree would never die. 
  * **tree of knowledge of [[:en:key-terms:good|good]] and [[:en:key-terms:evil|evil]]** - The fruit of this tree could enable a person to know the difference between good and evil. 
  * **knowledge** - To know or understand by personal experience
  * **[[:en:key-terms:good|good]] and [[:en:key-terms:evil|evil]]** - Evil is the opposite of good. Just as “good” refers to what is pleasing to God, “evil” refers to everything that is not pleasing to God. 
  * **[[:en:key-terms:death|die]]** - In this instance, he would die both physically and spiritually.
