====== Bless ======


===== "to bless" =====

(Level 1 to date) :: To desire for things go well” or “to give special positive status to something.

====Some Occurrences====

01-07 :: "...God saw that it was good & he __blessed__ them [fish & birds]"

01-15 :: ".... He __blessed__ them [Adam & Eve] and told them, 'Have many children and grandchildren'..."

01-16 :: "So God rested from all He had been doing. He __blessed__ the seventh day and made it holy, because on this day He rested from His work."

03-14 :: "God was happy with the sacrifice and __blessed__ Noah and his family."

04-04 :: "I [God] will make your [Abraham] name great. I will __bless__ those who __bless__ you and curse those who curse you. All families on earth will be __blessed__ because of you.”

04-06 :: "I [God] will __bless__ you [Abraham] and make your name great."

04-07 :: " Melchizedek __blessed__ Abram and said, 'May God Most High who owns heaven and earth bless Abram.'"

====Possible Level 2 Descriptions====

A blessing can be from God or a human

It can be described as
  * A promised enriching/prospering
  * The opposite of cursing
  * A promised gift
  * A reflection of divine approval
  * An empowering/A divine empowering
  * "a specific, recognizable manifestation of divine favor" (//The Great Doctrines of the Bible//, 280.)
  * To empower towards an end or purpose
