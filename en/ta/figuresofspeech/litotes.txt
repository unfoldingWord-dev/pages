====== [LIT] li·to·tes [līˈtōtēz] ======


==== Litotes is an emphatic statement made by saying that the opposite idea is not true. ====


Examples:


**1 Thessalonians 2:1** \\ 
For you yourselves know, brothers and sisters, about <font 9pt/arial;;#000000;;#ffffff>our coming to you–it has not proven to be purposeless</font> . NET\\ 
You know, brothers, that <font 9pt/arial;;#000000;;#ffffff>our visit to you was not a failure</font>. NIV\\ 
My fellow believers, you know that <font 9pt/arial;;#000000;;#ffffff>our time with you was very worthwhile/effective</font>. TFT


In this example of litotes, Paul emphasized that his visit with them was very successful by saying that it was not a failure.
