====== baptize ======


==== (to baptize, baptism) ====


===== Definition: =====

To baptize someone is to ritually wash that person with water. This act is called, "baptism."

    * John (the Baptist) baptized people as a sign of repentance from their sins. 
    * Christian baptism is a representation of our hearts being cleansed from sin by faith in Jesus Christ, and of our entrance into the Church.
    * Christians vary in the way they do baptism. Some pour the water on the person, and others put the person completely under the water.


[See also: **[[:en:key-terms:john-baptist|John (the Baptist)]]**, **[[:en:key-terms:repent|repentance]]**]

  ***[[:en:obs-training:frames:43-12|[43-12]]]** About 3,000 people believed what Peter said. They were **baptized** and were added to the people of God, which is the Church.
  ***[[:en:obs-training:frames:45-11|[45-11]]]** As they passed by some water, the Ethiopian said, “Look! There is some water! May I be **baptized**?”
  ***[[:en:obs-training:frames:46-05|[46-05]]]** Saul immediately regained his sight. Then Saul was **baptized**.
  ***[[:en:obs-training:frames:49-14|[49-14]]]** Jesus invites you to come to him, believe in him, and be **baptized**.
