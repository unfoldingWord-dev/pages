====== baptize ======


==== (to baptize, baptism) ====


===== Definition: =====


    * To be baptized means to ceremonially be purified by administering water.
    * Baptism is a means of symbolically identifying with Jesus' death and resurrection.
    * Christians do not all agree about how baptism should be done, whether pouring the water on the person or putting the person completely under the water.


[See also: **[[:en:key-terms:term|term]]**]

===== Examples from the Bible stories: =====

  ***[[:en:obs-training:frames:24-03|[24-03]]]** Some people repented and were **baptized** when they heard John’s message, but there were many religious leaders who came to be **baptized** without repenting and confessing their sins.
  ***[[:en:obs-training:frames:24-06|[24-06]]]** The next day, Jesus came to be **baptized** by John.
  ***[[:en:obs-training:frames:24-07|[24-07]]]** John said to Jesus, “I am not worthy to **baptize** you. You should **baptize** me instead.”
  ***[[:en:obs-training:frames:42-10|[42-10]]]** So go, make disciples of all people groups by **baptizing** them in the name of the Father, the Son, and the Holy Spirit and by teaching them to obey everything I have commanded you.”
  ***[[:en:obs-training:frames:43-11|[43-11]]]** Peter said, “Repent and be **baptized** every one of you in the name of Jesus Christ for the forgiveness of sins, and you will receive the gift of the Holy Spirit.”
  ***[[:en:obs-training:frames:43-12|[43-12]]]** About 3,000 people believed what Peter said. They were **baptized** and were added to the people of God, which is the Church.
  ***[[:en:obs-training:frames:45-11|[45-11]]]** As they passed by some water, the Ethiopian said, “Look! There is some water! May I be **baptized**?”
  ***[[:en:obs-training:frames:46-05|[46-05]]]** Saul immediately regained his sight. Then Saul was **baptized**.
  ***[[:en:obs-training:frames:49-14|[49-14]]]** Jesus invites you to come to him, believe in him, and be **baptized**.
