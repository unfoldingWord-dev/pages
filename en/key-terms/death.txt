====== Death ======


==== (death, die, dying, dead) ====


===== Definitions: =====


=== 1. Physical death ===


    * To die is to stop living. Death is the end of physical life.
    * Death is the point when the spirit of a person leaves his body.
    * Physical death is a consequence of sin. 


=== 2. Spiritual death ===


    * Spiritual death is the separation of a person from God.
    * Adam died spiritually when he disobeyed God. He became ashamed and tried to hide from God.
    * Every descendant of Adam is a **[[:en:key-terms:sin|sinner]]**, and is spiritually dead. God makes us spiritually alive again when we have faith in Jesus Christ.


Note: "death" is the opposite of "**[[:en:key-terms:life|life]]**". In the Bible, physical life and death are often compared to spiritual life and death.

===== Examples from the Bible stories: =====


[[:en:obs-training:frames:01-11|Two Trees [01-11]]] God told Adam that he could eat from any tree in the garden except from the tree of the knowledge of good and evil. If he ate from this tree, he would **die**.


[[:en:obs-training:frames:02-11|The Man is Cursed [02-11]]] "Then you will **die**, and your body will return to dirt."


The Brothers Reunited [07-10]  Then Isaac **died**, and Jacob and Esau buried him.


I Am the Resurrection [37-05]  "Whoever believes in me will live, even though he **dies**. Everyone who believes in me will never **die**."


The Crown of Life [50-13]  "They will never experience the second **death**, which is hell. Instead, they will live forever with God in perfect peace."
