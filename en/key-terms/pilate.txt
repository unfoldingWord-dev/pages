====== Pilate ======


===== Facts: =====

Pilate was the Roman governor who sentenced Jesus to death.

    * Because Pilate was the governor, he had the authority to put criminals to death.
    * The Jewish religious leaders wanted Pilate to crucify Jesus, so they lied and said that Jesus was a criminal.
    * Pilate realized that Jesus was not guilty, but he was afraid of the crowd, so he ordered his soldiers to crucify Jesus.

[See also: **[[:en:key-terms:rome|Rome]]**, **[[:en:key-terms:crucify|crucify]]**, **[[:en:key-terms:guilt|guilt]]**]


===== Examples from the Bible stories: =====

  ***[[:en:obs:notes:frames:39-09|[39-09]]]** Early the next morning, the Jewish leaders brought Jesus to **Pilate**, the Roman governor. They hoped that **Pilate** would condemn Jesus as guilty and sentenced him to be killed. **Pilate** asked Jesus, “Are you the King of the Jews?”
  ***[[:en:obs:notes:frames:39-10|[39-10]]]** **Pilate** said, “What is truth?”
  ***[[:en:obs:notes:frames:39-11|[39-11]]]** After speaking with Jesus, **Pilate** went out to the crowd and said, “I find no guilt in this man.” But the Jewish leaders and the crowd shouted, “Crucify him!” **Pilate** replied, “He is not guilty.” But they shouted even louder. Then **Pilate** said a third time, “He is not guilty!”
  ***[[:en:obs:notes:frames:39-12|[39-12]]]** **Pilate** became afraid that the crowd would begin to riot, so he ordered his soldiers to crucify Jesus.
  ***[[:en:obs:notes:frames:40-02|[40-02]]]** **Pilate** commanded that a sign be put above Jesus’ head that read, “King of the Jews.”
  ***[[:en:obs:notes:frames:41-02|[41-02]]]** **Pilate** said, “Take some soldiers and make the tomb as secure as you can.”
