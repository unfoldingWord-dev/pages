====== Holy ======


===== Definitions: =====


    * To be like **[[:en:key-terms:god|God]]** in character: morally clean, pure, sinless.
    * To be morally perfect, to be completely without sin.
    * To be set apart by God for a special purpose, such as a priest who must be ceremonially cleansed of sin to serve in a holy place.
    * To be separated from all wickedness, evil and moral filth.
    * Whatever is in the presence of God becomes holy as a result of His holiness, such as "holy ground."
    * Through Jesus' blood, God cleanses us of our sin and places on us Jesus' righteousness so that we appear before Him as holy.


[see also: **[[:en:key-terms:holy-spirit|Holy Spirit]]**]

===== Examples from the Bible stories: =====


[[:en:obs-training:frames:01-16|God Rests [01-16]]] God blessed the seventh day and made it **holy**, because on this day He rested from His work.


[[:en:obs-training:frames:09-12|The Burning Bush [09-12]]] "You are standing on **holy** ground."


[[:en:obs-training:frames:13-01|God Calls Israel  [13-02]]] "If you will obey My voice and keep My covenant, you will be My prized possession, a kingdom of priests, and a **holy** nation."


[[:en:obs-training:frames:13-05|The Commandments [13-05]]] "Remember to keep the Sabbath day **holy**."


[[:en:obs-training:frames:50-02|The Good News [50-02]]] Christians are to live **holy** and godly lives.
