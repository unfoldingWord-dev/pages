====== Holy ======


===== Definitions: =====

  * To be holy is to be like **[[:en:key-terms:god|God]]** in character--clean, pure, sinless.
  * Holiness is to be morally perfect, to be completely without sin, and separated to God and separated from all wickedness, evil, and moral filth. 
  * To be set apart by God, or to God, for a special purpose, such as a priest who must be ceremonially cleansed of sin to serve in a holy place. 
  * Things that are in the presence of God become holy as a result of His holiness.

[see also: **[[:en:key-terms:holy-spirit|Holy Spirit]]**]


===== Examples from the Bible stories: =====


[[:en:obs-training:frames:01-16|God Rests [01-16]]] God blessed the seventh day and made it **holy**, because on this day He rested from His work.


The Burning Bush [09-12] "You are standing on **holy** ground."


God Calls Israel [13-02] "If you will obey My voice and keep My covenant, you will be My prized possession, a kingdom of priests, and a **holy** nation."


The Commandments [13-05] "Remember to keep the Sabbath day **holy**."


The Good News [50-02] Christians are to live **holy** and godly lives.
