====== God's law ======


==== (term, other) ====


===== Definitions Facts: =====

  * God's law is the collection of all the commands and instructions that he gave to his people Israel.


[See also: **[[:en:key-terms:term|term]]**]


===== Examples from the Bible stories: =====

  ***[[:en:obs-training:frames:13-07|[13-07]]]** God also gave many other **laws** and rules to follow. If the people obeyed these **laws**, God promised that he would bless and protect them. If they disobeyed them, God would punish them.
  ***[[:en:obs-training:frames:13-09|[13-09]]]** Anyone who disobeyed **God’s law** could bring an animal to the Tent of Meeting as a sacrifice to God.
  ***[[:en:obs-training:frames:15-13|[15-13]]]** Then Joshua renewed the covenant that God had made with the Israelites at Sinai. The people promised to remain faithful to God and follow **his laws**.
  ***[[:en:obs-training:frames:16-01|[16-01]]]** After Joshua died, the Israelites disobeyed God and did not drive out the rest of the Canaanites or obey **God’s laws**.
  ***[[:en:obs-training:frames:21-05|[21-05]]]** In the New Covenant, God would write **his law** on the people’s hearts, the people would know God, they would be his people, and God would forgive their sins.
  ***[[:en:obs-training:frames:27-01|[27-01]]]** Jesus answered, “What is written in **God’s law**?”
  ***[[:en:obs-training:frames:28-01|[28-01]]]** Jesus said to him, “Why do you ask me about what is good? There is only One who is good, and that is God. But if you want to have eternal life, obey **God’s laws**.”
