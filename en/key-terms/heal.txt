====== heal ======


==== (term, other) ====


===== Definitions: =====

  * To heal is to cause a person's wounds or disease to be made better.


[See also: **[[:en:key-terms:term|term]]**]


===== Examples from the Bible stories: =====

  ***[[:en:obs-training:frames:19-14|[19-14]]]** One of the miracles happened to Naaman, an enemy commander, who had a horrible skin disease. He had heard of Elisha so he went and asked Elisha to **heal** him.
  ***[[:en:obs-training:frames:21-10|[21-10]]]** He (Isaiah) also predicted that the Messiah would **heal** sick people, including those who could not hear, see, speak, or walk.
  ***[[:en:obs-training:frames:26-06|[26-06]]]** “And when the prophet Elisha was alive, there were many people in Israel with skin diseases. But Elisha did not **heal** any of them. He only **healed** Naaman, a commander of Israel’s enemies.”
  ***[[:en:obs-training:frames:26-08|[26-08]]]** They brought all the sick people, including those who could not see, walk, hear, or speak, and Jesus **healed** them.
  ***[[:en:obs-training:frames:32-14|[32-14]]]** She heard how Jesus had **healed** many sick people and thought to herself, “If I only touch Jesus’ clothes, then I, too, will be **healed**!”
  ***[[:en:obs-training:frames:44-03|[44-03]]]** The man was immediately **healed**, and he began walking and jumping around, praising God.
  ***[[:en:obs-training:frames:44-08|[44-08]]]** Peter responded, “It is by the power of Jesus the Messiah that we **healed** this man.”
  ***[[:en:obs-training:frames:49-02|[49-02]]]** Jesus did many miracles proving he is God. He walked on water, calmed storms, **healed** many sick people, drove out demons, raised the dead to life, and fed 5,000 people with five loaves of bread and two fish.
