====== judge ======


==== (to judge, judgment) ====

===== Definition: =====


To judge is to decide whether something or someone is legally or morally right or wrong. This decision is called a "judgment."

    * When God judges sin, it means he condemns it as evil.
    * The "judgment of God" may express his decision to condemn something or someone as sinful.
    * The "judgment of God" also often refers to his punishment of sinners.

[See also: **[[:en:key-terms:justice|justice]]**]


===== Examples from the Bible stories: =====

  ***[[:en:obs-training:frames:19-16|[19-16]]]** The prophets warned the people that if they did not stop doing evil and start obeying God, then God would **judge** them as guilty, and he would punish them.
  ***[[:en:obs-training:frames:21-08|[21-08]]]** A king is someone who rules over a kingdom and **judges** the people. The Messiah would come would be the perfect king who would sit on the throne of his ancestor David. He would reign over the whole world forever, and who would always **judge** honestly and make the right decisions.
  ***[[:en:obs-training:frames:39-04|[39-04]]]** The high priest tore his clothes in anger and shouted to the other religious leaders, “We do not need any more witnesses! You have heard him say that he is the Son of God. What is your **judgment**?”
  ***[[:en:obs-training:frames:50-14|[50-14]]]** But those who do not believe in Jesus will be **judged** by God and thrown into hell forever.
