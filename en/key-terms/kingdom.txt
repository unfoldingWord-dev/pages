====== kingdom ======


===== Definition: =====

A kingdom is a group of people ruled by a king.

  * A kingdom can be of any geographical size; a king might govern a nation or country or only one city.
  * "Kingdom" also refers to the ruling or governing power that a king exercises over a group of people.
  * God's kingdom refers to his rule over everything.
  * Satan also temporarily rules over many things. This is "Satan's kingdom" which will be destroyed by God.

[See also: **[[:en:key-terms:king|king]]**, **[[:en:key-terms:kingdom-of-god|Kingdom of God]]**]


===== Examples from the Bible stories: =====

  ***[[:en:obs:notes:frames:13-02|[13-02]]]** God said to Moses and the people of Israel, “If you will obey me and keep my covenant, you will be my prized possession, a **kingdom** of priests, and a holy nation.”
  ***[[:en:obs:notes:frames:18-04|[18-04]]]** God was angry with Solomon and, as a punishment for Solomon’s unfaithfulness, he promised to divide the nation of Israel in two **kingdoms** after Solomon’s death.
  ***[[:en:obs:notes:frames:18-07|[18-07]]]** Ten of the tribes of the nation of Israel rebelled against Rehoboam. Only two tribes remained faithful to him. These two tribes became the **kingdom** of Judah.
  ***[[:en:obs:notes:frames:18-08|[18-08]]]** The other ten tribes of the nation of Israel that rebelled against Rehoboam appointed a man named Jeroboam to be their king. They set up their **kingdom** in the northern part of the land and were called the **kingdom** of Israel.
  ***[[:en:obs:notes:frames:21-08|[21-08]]]** A king is someone who rules over a **kingdom** and judges the people.
