====== kingdom ======


===== Definitions: =====

A kingdom is a group of people ruled by a king.

  * A kingdom can be of any geographical size; a king might govern a nation or country or only one city.
  * "Kingdom" also refers to the ruling or governing power that a king exercises over a group of people.

[See also: **[[:en:key-terms:king|king]]**, **[[:en:key-terms:kingdom-of-god|Kingdom of God]]**]
===== Examples from the Bible stories: =====


  ***[[:en:obs-training:frames:13-02|[13-02]]]** God said to Moses and the people of Israel, “If you will obey my voice and keep my covenant, you will be my prized possession, a **kingdom** of priests, and a holy nation.”
  ***[[:en:obs-training:frames:18-04|[18-04]]]** God was angry with Solomon and, as a punishment for Solomon’s unfaithfulness, he promised to divide the **kingdom** of Israel in two after Solomon’s death.
  ***[[:en:obs-training:frames:18-07|[18-07]]]** Ten of the tribes of Israel rebelled against Rehoboam. Only two tribes remained faithful to him. These two tribes became the **kingdom** of Judah.
  ***[[:en:obs-training:frames:18-08|[18-08]]]** The other ten tribes that rebelled against Rehoboam made a man named Jeroboam their king. They set up their **kingdom** in the northern part of the land and were called the **kingdom** of Israel.
  ***[[:en:obs-training:frames:21-08|[21-08]]]** A king is someone who rules over a **kingdom** and judges the people.
