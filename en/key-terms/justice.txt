====== justice ======


==== (justice, justly) ====


===== Definition: =====

Justice is right or reasonable behavior or treatment.

  * Laws, actions, and decisions that are just are morally right and fair.
  * To rule justly is to make decisions that are morally right and to treat fairly those people over whom one rules.  


[See also: **[[:en:key-terms:judge|judge]]**]


===== Examples from the Bible stories: =====

  ***[[:en:obs:notes:frames:17-09|[17-09]]]** David ruled with **justice** and faithfulness for many years, and God blessed him.
  ***[[:en:obs:notes:frames:18-13|[18-13]]]** Some of these kings (of Judah) were good men who ruled **justly** and worshiped God.
  ***[[:en:obs:notes:frames:19-16|[19-16]]]** They (the prophets) all told the people to stop worshiping idols and to start showing **justice** and mercy to others.
  ***[[:en:obs:notes:frames:50-17|[50-17]]]** He (Jesus) will rule his Kingdom with peace and **justice**, and he will be with his people forever.
