====== save ======


==== (save, salvation, saved) ====


===== Definition: =====

To save someone or something is to rescue that person or thing from something bad.

  * In a physical sense, people can be saved from harm, danger, or death.
  * In a spiritual sense, if a person has been "saved," it means that through Jesus' death on the cross, God has forgiven him and rescued him from being punished for his sin.
  * Only God can save people from being punished for their sin.


===== Examples from the Bible stories: =====

  ***[[:en:obs:notes:frames:09-08|[09-08]]]** Moses tried to **save** his fellow Israelite.
  ***[[:en:obs:notes:frames:11-02|[11-02]]]** God provided a way to **save** the firstborn son of anyone who believed in him.
  ***[[:en:obs:notes:frames:12-05|[12-05]]]** Moses told the Israelites, "Stop being afraid! God will fight for you today and **save** you."
  ***[[:en:obs:notes:frames:12-13|[12-13]]]** The Israelites sang many songs to celebrate their new freedom and to praise God because he **saved** them from the Egyptian army.
  ***[[:en:obs:notes:frames:16-17|[16-17]]]** This pattern repeated many times: the Israelites would sin, God would punish them, they would repent, and God would send a deliverer to **save** them.
  ***[[:en:obs:notes:frames:49-12|[49-12]]]** No one can be **saved** by doing good things. 
  ***[[:en:obs:notes:frames:49-13|[49-13]]]** Anyone who believes in Jesus and receives him as their Master will be **saved**. But whoever does not believe will be rejected.
