====== Passover ======


==== (Passover, Passover Feast) ====


===== Definitions: =====

When God killed all the firstborn sons of the Egyptians, he **passed over** the houses of the Israelites and did not kill their sons.

  * God told the Israelites to eat this meal every year in order to remember and celebrate that how God "passed over" their houses and how he set them free from slavery in Egypt.
  * The Passover Feast includes a special meal that reminds people of the meal they ate that night.



===== Examples from the Bible stories: =====


  ***[[:en:obs-training:frames:12-14|[12-14]]]**  God commanded the Israelites to remember his victory over the Egyptians and their deliverance from slavery by celebrating the **Passover** every year.
  ***[[:en:obs-training:frames:38-01|[38-01]]]**  It was the time of the **Passover** Feast when the Jews remembered how God had saved them from slavery in Egypt many centuries earlier.
  ***[[:en:obs-training:frames:38-04|[38-04]]]**  Jesus celebrated the **Passover** Feast with his disciples.
  ***[[:en:obs-training:frames:48-09|[48-09]]]**  When God saw the blood, he passed over their houses and did not kill their firstborn sons. This is called the **Passover**.
  ***[[:en:obs-training:frames:48-10|[48-10]]]**  Jesus is our **Passover** Lamb. He was perfect and sinless and was killed at the time of the **Passover** Feast.
