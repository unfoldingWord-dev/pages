====== beg ======


==== (to beg, beggar) ====


===== Definition: =====

"To beg" is to ask for something earnestly, urgently, and often humbly.

  * Often people beg when they think that the  other person might not want to give them what they ask for.
  * "Beg” can also mean to ask for money; a beggar is someone who sits or stands in a public place to ask people for money. 


===== Examples from the Bible stories: =====

  ***[[:en:obs:notes:frames:10-04|[10-04]]]** God sent frogs all over Egypt. Pharaoh **begged** Moses to take away the frogs.
  ***[[:en:obs:notes:frames:29-08|[29-08]]]** “The king called the servant and said, ‘You wicked servant! I forgave your debt because you **begged** me.”
  ***[[:en:obs:notes:frames:32-07|[32-07]]]** The demons **begged** Jesus, “Please do not send us out of this region!” There was a herd of pigs feeding on a nearby hill. So, the demons **begged** Jesus, “Please send us into the pigs instead!”
  ***[[:en:obs:notes:frames:32-10|[32-10]]]** The man who used to have the demons **begged** to go along with Jesus.
  ***[[:en:obs:notes:frames:35-11|[35-11]]]** His father came out and **begged** him to come and celebrate with them, but he refused.”
  ***[[:en:obs:notes:frames:44-01|[44-01]]]** One day, Peter and John were going to the Temple. As they approached the Temple gate, they saw a crippled man who was **begging** for money.
