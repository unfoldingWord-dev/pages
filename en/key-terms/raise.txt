====== raise ======


==== (to raise, to rise, risen) ====


===== Definition: =====

  * To resurrect, or bring a dead person back to life
  * 

[See also: **[[:en:key-terms:resurrection|resurrection]]**]


===== Examples from the Bible stories: =====

  ***[[:en:obs-training:frames:21-14|[21-14]]]** Not only would the Messiah die, but the prophets foretold that God would **raise** him from the dead.
  ***[[:en:obs-training:frames:36-07|[36-07]]]** As Jesus and the three disciples went back down the mountain, Jesus said to them, “Do not tell anyone about what happened here until after I have been **raised** from the dead.”
  ***[[:en:obs-training:frames:41-05|[41-05]]]** Jesus is not here. He has **risen** from the dead, just like he said he would!
  ***[[:en:obs-training:frames:43-07|[43-07]]]** “But God **raised** him to life again to fulfill the prophecy which says, ‘You will not let your Holy One rot in the grave.’ God **raised** this Jesus from the dead. We are witnesses.”
  ***[[:en:obs-training:frames:44-05|[44-05]]]** “You killed the author of life, but God **raised** him from the dead.”
  ***[[:en:obs-training:frames:44-08|[44-08]]]** Peter responded, “It is by the power of Jesus the Messiah that we healed this man. You crucified him, but God **raised** him to life again!
  ***[[:en:obs-training:frames:48-04|[48-04]]]** God promised that one of Eve’s descendants would crush Satan’s head, and Satan would wound his heel. This meant that Satan would kill Jesus, but God would **raise** him to life after three days.
  ***[[:en:obs-training:frames:49-02|[49-02]]]** He (Jesus) walked on water, calmed storms, healed many sick people, drove out demons, **raised** the dead to life, and fed 5,000 people with five loaves of bread and two fish.
  ***[[:en:obs-training:frames:49-12|[49-12]]]** Your sins can only be washed away if you believe that Jesus is the Son of God, that he died on the cross in your place, and that God **raised** him to life again.
