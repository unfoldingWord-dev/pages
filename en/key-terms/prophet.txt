====== prophet ======


==== (prophet, prophecy, prophesied) ====


===== Definitions: =====

A prophet is a person who speaks God's messages to people. He tells them what God wants them to know. God is the one who chooses and appoints the prophet to do this.

  * "Prophet" could be translated as "God's spokesman."
  * A prophecy is the message that God tells the prophet to speak. 
  * Many prophecies that were recorded in the Old Testament of the Bible were fulfilled in the New Testament. Others are still being fulfilled.
  * To prophesy is the act of communicating God's message.
  * When prophets in the Bible prophesied, often they would tell people about things that God had said would happen in the future. Prophets also warned people to turn away from their sins and obey God. 
  * There were also false prophets who claimed to speak messages from false gods such as Baal.
  

===== Examples from the Bible stories: =====


  ***[[:en:obs-training:frames:12-12|[12-12]]]** When the Israelites saw that the Egyptians were dead, they believed in God and trusted that Moses was a **prophet** of God. 
  ***[[:en:obs-training:frames:14-14|[14-14]]]** God also promised Moses that one day He would raise up another **prophet** like Moses. 
  ***[[:en:obs-training:frames:17-13|[17-13]]]** God was not pleased with what David had done, so he sent the **prophet** Nathan to show David his sin.
  ***[[:en:obs-training:frames:19-01|[19-01]]]** Throughout the history of the Israelites, God sent them **prophets**. The **prophets** heard messages from God and then told the people God's messages.
  ***[[:en:obs-training:frames:19-06|[19-06]]]** All of Israel, including the 450 **prophets** of Baal, came to Mount Carmel.
  ***[[:en:obs-training:frames:19-17|[19-17]]]** Most of the time, the people did not listen to God. They often mistreated the **prophets** and sometimes even killed them.
  ***[[:en:obs-training:frames:21-09|[21-09]]]** Isaiah **prophesied** that the Messiah would be born from a virgin.
  ***[[:en:obs-training:frames:40-03|[40-03]]]** When they did this, they fulfilled a **prophecy** which said, "They divided my garments among them, and gambled for my clothing."
  ***[[:en:obs-training:frames:43-05|[43-05]]]** "This is a fulfillment of the **prophecy** made by Joel which says, 'In the last days, I will pour out my Spirit.'"
  ***[[:en:obs-training:frames:43-07|[43-07]]]** "But God raised him to life again to fulfill the **prophecy** which says, 'You will not let your Holy One rot in the grave.'"
  ***[[:en:obs-training:frames:48-12|[48-12]]]** Like Moses, Jesus is a **prophet**, but Jesus is the greatest **prophet**. He is the Word of God.
