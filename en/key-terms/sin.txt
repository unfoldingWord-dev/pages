====== Sin ======


==== (sin, sinful, sinner) ====


===== Definitions: =====


    * Anything that is opposed to the way **[[:en:key-terms:god|God]]** desires for us to think and act.
    * Anything that is in opposition to God's character.
    * The world was created without sin, but the first people, **[[:en:key-terms:adam|Adam ]]** and **[[:en:key-terms:eve|Eve]]**, sinned by eating the fruit that God told them not to eat.
    * Sin is passed from parent to child through the entire human race.
    * God must judge sin.

[see also: **[[:en:key-terms:evil|evil]]**, **[[:en:key-terms:wicked|wicked]]**, **[[:en:key-terms:disobey|disobey]]**]


===== Examples from the Bible stories: =====

[[:en:obs-training:frames:02-01|Man in God's Image [03-15]]] "God said, 'I will never again curse the ground because of humans or destroy the world with a flood, even though humans are **sinful** from their youth.'”

[[:en:obs-training:frames:13-12|The Golden Calf [13-12]]] [13-12]  "...God was very angry with their **sin** and planned to destroy them."

[[:en:obs-training:frames:19-16|The Prophets Warn the People [19-16]]] "The prophets warned the people that if they did not turn from their **sins** and start living obedient lives, then God’s judgment would come, and He would punish them."

[[:en:obs-training:frames:20-01|Isreal Sins [20-01]]] "The nations of Israel and Judah both **sinned** against God. They broke the Covenant that God made with them at Sinai."
