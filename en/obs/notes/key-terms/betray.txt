====== betray ======


==== (to betray, betrayer, betrayed) ====


===== Definition: =====


To betray someone is to gain that person's trust, but then to act in a way that deceives that person or is harmful to him in some way.

    * Judas was "the betrayer" because he told the Jewish leaders how to capture Jesus.
    * The betrayal by Judas was especially evil because Judas was an apostle of Jesus, because Judas betrayed Jesus for money, and because the betrayal ended with the unjust execution of Jesus.


[See also: **[[en:obs:notes:key-terms:judas|Judas]]**, **[[en:obs:notes:key-terms:jewish-leaders|Jewish leaders]]**, **[[en:obs:notes:key-terms:apostle|apostle]]**]


===== Examples from the Bible stories: =====

  ***[[:en:obs:notes:frames:21-11|[21-11]]]** Other prophets foretold that those who killed the Messiah would gamble for his clothes and he would be **betrayed** by a friend. The prophet Zechariah foretold that the friend would be paid thirty silver coins as payment for **betraying** the Messiah.
  ***[[:en:obs:notes:frames:38-02|[38-02]]]** After Jesus and the disciples arrived in Jerusalem, Judas went to the Jewish leaders and offered to **betray** Jesus to them in exchange for money. 
  ***[[:en:obs:notes:frames:38-03|[38-03]]]** The Jewish leaders, led by the high priest, paid Judas thirty silver coins to **betray** Jesus. 
  ***[[:en:obs:notes:frames:38-06|[38-06]]]** Then Jesus said to the disciples, "One of you will **betray** me." ... Jesus said, "The person to whom I give this piece of bread is the **betrayer**."
  ***[[:en:obs:notes:frames:38-13|[38-13]]]** When he returned the third time, Jesus said, "Wake up! My **betrayer** is here."
  ***[[:en:obs:notes:frames:38-14|[38-14]]]** Then Jesus said, "Judas, do you **betray** me with a kiss?"
  ***[[:en:obs:notes:frames:39-08|[39-08]]]** Meanwhile, Judas, the **betrayer**, saw that the Jewish leaders had condemned Jesus to die. He became full of sorrow and went away and killed himself.
