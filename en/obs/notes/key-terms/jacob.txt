====== Jacob ======


===== Facts: =====

Jacob was the second of the twin sons that were born to Isaac and Rebekah. The first twin born was Esau.

    * Jacob's name means "deceiver" or "trickster." 
    * Jacob was clever and deceptive. He found ways to take both the firstborn inheritance rights and the blessing from his older brother, Esau.
    * Esau was angry and planned to kill him, so Jacob left his homeland. But he later returned and lived peacefully with his brother.
    * God changes Jacob's name to Israel, which means, "He struggles with God." 
    * God kept his covenant to Abraham and his descendants through Abraham's son, Isaac, and Isaac's son, Jacob.
    * Jacob had twelve sons. Their descendants became the twelve tribes of Israel.

===== Examples from the Bible stories: =====

  ***[[:en:obs:notes:frames:07-01|[07-01]]]** As the boys grew up, Rebekah loved **Jacob**, but Isaac loved Esau. **Jacob** loved to stay at home, but Esau loved to hunt.
  ***[[:en:obs:notes:frames:07-07|[07-07]]]**  **Jacob** lived there for many years, and during that time he married and had twelve sons and a daughter. God made him very wealthy.
  ***[[:en:obs:notes:frames:07-08|[07-08]]]** After twenty years away from his home in Canaan, **Jacob** returned there with his family, his servants, and all his herds of animals.
  ***[[:en:obs:notes:frames:07-10|[07-10]]]** The covenant promises God had promised to Abraham and then to Isaac now passed on to **Jacob**.
  ***[[:en:obs:notes:frames:08-01|[08-01]]]** Many years later, when **Jacob** was an old man, he sent his favorite son, Joseph, to check on his brothers who were taking care of the herds.
