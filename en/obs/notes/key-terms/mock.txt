====== mock ======


===== Definition: =====


To deride or make fun of someone, especially  in a cruel way.

    * Mocking often involves taunting someone and laughing at them.
    * To mock someone is to pretend to honor them, but in a way that actually shows contempt for them.
    * A person can also mock someone by imitating that person's words or actions with the intent to embarrass them.


===== Examples from the Bible stories: =====

  ***[[:en:obs:notes:frames:21-12|[21-12]]]** Isaiah prophesied that people would spit on, **mock**, and beat the Messiah. 
  ***[[:en:obs:notes:frames:39-05|[39-05]]]** The Jewish leaders all answered the high priest, "He deserves to die!" Then they blindfolded Jesus, spit on him, hit him, and **mocked** him.
  ***[[:en:obs:notes:frames:39-12|[39-12]]]** The soldiers whipped Jesus, and put a royal robe and a crown made of thorns on him. Then they **mocked** him by saying, "Look, the King of the Jews!"
  ***[[:en:obs:notes:frames:40-04|[40-04]]]** Jesus was crucified between two robbers. One of them **mocked** Jesus, but the other said, "Do you have no fear of God?"
  ***[[:en:obs:notes:frames:40-05|[40-05]]]** The Jewish leaders and the other people in the crowd **mocked** Jesus. They said to him, "If you are the Son of God, come down from the cross and save yourself! Then we will believe you."
