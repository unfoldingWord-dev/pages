====== faithful======


==== (faithful, faithfulness) ====


===== Definition: =====

Being faithful (faithfulness) means being loyal to a person or living according to a set of beliefs.  

  * A faithful person can be depended on to always keep his promises.
  * To be faithful to someone means to be loyal to that person.
  * A faithful person remains dedicated to a task, even when it is long and difficult.
  * Faithfulness is the consistent practice of doing what we say we will do, or what we believe God wants us to do.
  * God is faithful to us by keeping His promises to us.
  * We are faithful to God when we persevere in obeying Him.

The opposite of "faithful" is "unfaithful."

  ***[[:en:obs:notes:frames:08-05|[08-05]]]** Even in prison, Joseph remained **faithful** to God, and God blessed him.

  ***[[:en:obs:notes:frames:15-13|[15-13]]]** The people promised to remain **faithful** to God and follow his laws.
  ***[[:en:obs:notes:frames:17-09|[17-09]]]**  David ruled with justice and **faithfulness** for many years, and God blessed him. However, toward the end of his life he sinned terribly against God.
  ***[[:en:obs:notes:frames:18-04|[18-04]]]** God was angry with Solomon and, as a punishment for Solomon's **unfaithfulness**, he promised to divide the nation of Israel into two kingdoms after Solomon's death.
  ***[[:en:obs:notes:frames:35-12|[35-12]]]** "The older son said to his father, 'All these years I have worked **faithfully** for you!"
  ***[[:en:obs:notes:frames:49-17|[49-17]]]** But God is **faithful** and says that if you confess your sins, he will forgive you. 
  ***[[:en:obs:notes:frames:50-04|[50-04]]]** If you remain **faithful** to me to the end, then God will save you."
