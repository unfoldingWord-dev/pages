====== Uriah ======


===== Facts: =====

Uriah was a righteous man and one of King David's best soldiers.

  * Uriah had a very beautiful wife named Bathsheba.
  * David committed adultery with Uriah's wife, and she became pregnant with David's child.
  * David caused Uriah to be killed in battle in order to cover up his sin. David then married Bathsheba.


[See also: **[[en:obs:notes:key-terms:David|David]]**, **[[en:obs:notes:key-terms:Bathsheba|Bathsheba]]**]


===== Examples from the Bible stories: =====

  ***[[:en:obs:notes:frames:17-12|[17-12]]]** Bathsheba's husband, a man named **Uriah**, was one of David's best soldiers. David called **Uriah** back from the battle and told him to go be with his wife. But **Uriah** refused to go home while the rest of the soldiers were in battle. So David sent **Uriah** back to the battle and told the general to place him where the enemy was strongest so that he would be killed.
  ***[[:en:obs:notes:frames:17-13|[17-13]]]** After **Uriah** was killed, David married Bathsheba.
