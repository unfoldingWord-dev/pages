====== sheep ======


===== Definition: =====

A sheep is a medium-sized animal with four legs that has wool all over its body.

  * People eat meat from sheep and use their wool to make clothing and other things.
  * Sheep are very trusting, weak and timid. They are easily influenced to wander away. They need a shepherd to lead them in the right direction and to provide them with food, water and shelter.
  * In the Bible, people are compared to sheep who have God as their shepherd.
  
[See also: **[[en:obs:notes:key-terms:shepherd|shepherd]]**, **[[en:obs:notes:key-terms:lamb|lamb]]**]


===== Examples from the Bible stories: =====

  ***[[:en:obs:notes:frames:09-12|[09-12]]]** One day while Moses was taking care of his **sheep**, he saw a bush that was on fire.
  ***[[:en:obs:notes:frames:17-02|[17-02]]]** David was a shepherd from the town of Bethlehem. At different times while he was watching his father's **sheep**, David had killed both a lion and a bear that had attacked the **sheep**.
  ***[[:en:obs:notes:frames:30-03|[30-03]]]** To Jesus, these people were like **sheep** without a shepherd.
  ***[[:en:obs:notes:frames:38-08|[38-08]]]** Jesus said, "All of you will all abandon me tonight. It is written, 'I will strike the shepherd and all the **sheep** will be scattered.'"
