====== bless ======


==== (bless, blessed, blessing) ====


===== Definition: =====

To "bless" someone or something is to state a desire for positive and beneficial things to happen to them.

    * To cause beneficial things to happen to someone or something.
    * A father would often pronounce a formal blessing on his children.
    * When God blesses something he causes it to be fruitful and to have a positive effect on others. 

The opposite of "bless" is "curse."

[See also: **[[en:obs:notes:key-terms:curse|curse]]**]

===== Examples from the Bible stories: =====


  ***[[:en:obs:notes:frames:01-07|[01-07]]]** God saw that it was good and he **blessed** them.
  ***[[:en:obs:notes:frames:01-15|[01-15]]]** God made Adam and Eve in his own image. He **blessed** them and told them, "Have many children and grandchildren and fill the earth."
  ***[[:en:obs:notes:frames:01-16|[01-16]]]** So God rested from all he had been doing. He **blessed** the seventh day and made it holy, because on this day he rested from his work.
  ***[[:en:obs:notes:frames:04-04|[04-04]]]** "I will make your name great. I will **bless** those who **bless** you and curse those who curse you. All families on earth will be **blessed** because of you."
  ***[[:en:obs:notes:frames:04-07|[04-07]]]** Melchizedek **blessed** Abram and said, "May God Most High who owns heaven and earth **bless** Abram."
  ***[[:en:obs:notes:frames:07-03|[07-03]]]** Isaac wanted to give his **blessing** to Esau.
  ***[[:en:obs:notes:frames:08-05|[08-05]]]** Even in prison, Joseph remained faithful to God, and God **blessed** him.
