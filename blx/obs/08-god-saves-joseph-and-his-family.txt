====== 8. God Saves Joseph and His Family ======

{{:en:obs:obs-08-01.jpg?nolink&640x360}}

Many years later, when Jacob was an old man, he sent his favorite son, Joseph, to check on his brothers who were taking care of the herds.

Sa matuwa yinay Jacob, dilag gisay allu ya in-utus na yay gisay anak na ya kakaidwan na ya naglagyun Jose. Sinabi na kana, “Anak ku, lakwên mu silan elêwên ya patêl mun magpastul.” 

{{:en:obs:obs-08-02.jpg?nolink&640x360}}

Joseph’s brothers hated him because their father loved him most and because Joseph had dreamed that he would be their ruler. When Joseph found his brothers, they kidnapped him and sold him to some slave traders.

Silay patêl na, tanda la ya si Jose ya kakaidwan nan bapa la buy tanda la êt ya taynêp nan Jose ya sabay ya mag-ari kalla. Kabay dilag silan sakit nakêm kan Jose.

{{:en:obs:obs-08-03.jpg?nolink&640x360}}

Before they returned home, the brothers tore Joseph’s clothes and dipped them in blood. Then they showed the clothes to their father and lied to him saying a wild animal had killed Joseph. Jacob was very sad.

Amêsên, sa inlumatêng si Jose sa mani patêl na, kinwa la ya buy inkulbut la yina. Nuwa dilag manduman ya manaliw, kingwa la ya sa kalalê-lalêan. Ta insaliw la ya sa mani manaliw. Buy gintan la yina papakun sa Egipto. Amêsên, silay patêl na, sa nuli sila sa bali la, inpakit la ya yaming nan Jose kan bapa la, ya sinabi la, “Bapay, nakit yan ya yaming nan Jose sa dan.” Buy si Jacob, sinabi na, “Siguru dilag gisay saysay ya kakaywan ya namati kana.” Buy nilungkut yina sadya.

{{:en:obs:obs-08-04.jpg?nolink&640x360}}

The slave traders took Joseph to Egypt. Egypt was a large, powerful country located beside the Nile River. The slave traders sold Joseph as a slave to a wealthy government official. Joseph served his master well, and God blessed Joseph.

Amêsên, silay manaliw, sa pamakalatêng sila sa Egipto, insaliw la yina man si Jose sa gisay mabandi, ya naglagyun Potiphar.

{{:en:obs:obs-08-05.jpg?nolink&640x360}}

His master’s wife tried to sleep with Joseph, but Joseph refused to sin against God in this way. She became angry and had Joseph arrested and sent to prison. Even in prison, Joseph remained faithful to God, and God blessed him.

Sa anti yina baydu si Jose sa Egipto, mapagkatiwalan ya sa amung na. Nuwa ya asawa nan amung na, labay na yan makikanayun kan Jose. Amêsên, si Jose, a na labay ya mangasalanan kan Bapan Namalyari. Buy si asawa nan Potiphar, sinangkan na si Jose sa ayin kapêtêgan. Kabay naisukul ya. Nuwa agyan anti ya sa sukulan, mapagkatiwalan yan êt murin, ta pagpalên na yan Bapan Namalyari.

{{:en:obs:obs-08-06.jpg?nolink&640x360}}

Years later, Joseph was still in prison, even though he had done nothing wrong. The king of Egypt, who was called the Pharaoh, had two dreams that disturbed him greatly. None of his advisors could tell him the meaning of the dreams.

Sabaytun panawun, ya Puun Paraun ya mag-ari pun sa Egipto, nanaynêp ya. Buy naguluwan ya ta ayin maipatanda kana nu sabêt labay sabin taynêp na. 

{{:en:obs:obs-08-07.jpg?nolink&640x360}}

God had given Joseph the ability to interpret dreams, so Pharaoh had Joseph brought to him from the prison. Joseph interpreted the dreams for him and said, “God is going to send seven years of good harvests followed by seven years of famine.”

Buy si Jose, tanda nay labay sabin mani taynêp. Ta pan-ipatanda nan Bapan Namalyari kana. Buy si Puun Paraun, nabalitan na ya si Jose ya talagan mamitutung labay sabin un mani taynêp. Kabay inpabêg na si Jose sa sukulan, ta labay nan Puun Paraun ya ipatanda nan Jose ya labay sabin un taynêp na. Buy sa anti yina Jose sa arapan na, sinabi na kan Jose, “Jose, ipatanda mu dayi kangku ya labay sabin un taynêp ku.” Buy sinabi nan Jose kana, “Panggalangên nan Puun Paraun, inpatanda nan Bapan Namalyari kangku ya labay sabin taynêp mu. Buy paradi ya labay sabin. Sa lalên un pituy tawun, mangêd ya pupul baydi sa Egipto, nuwa dilag êt kasunul sa lalên un pitu tawun ya ayin yinan pupul.”

{{:en:obs:obs-08-08.jpg?nolink&640x360}}

Pharaoh was so impressed with Joseph that he made him the second most powerful man in all of Egypt!

Si Puun Paraun, sên nalêngê nay labay sabin un taynêp, natula ya kan Jose. Kabay dinyag na yan ikaluwan pinakamatag-ay ya mamaala baydu sa bansan Egipto.

{{:en:obs:obs-08-09.jpg?nolink&640x360}}

During the seven years of good harvests, Joseph told the people to store up large amounts of food. Then when the seven years of famine came, Joseph sold the food to the people so they would have enough to eat.

Ya kal-atan un pupul sa Egipto sa lalên un pituy tawun, si Jose ya mamantay un natagan buy siya êt murin ya mamaala sa mani nanipun un kaatag ga pupul baydu ta amên la maisimpan ya kaatag ya pupul sa Egipto. Buy sa naabut yinay kasunul ya lalên un pituy tawun, magkasakit ya mani tawu.

{{:en:obs:obs-08-10.jpg?nolink&640x360}}

The famine was severe not only in Egypt, but also in Canaan where Jacob and his family lived.

Ta layangên silay nan sadya. Alwan kay sa Egipto ya matanam un sadya ya layang. Ta agyan êt sa Canaan ya lugal la inpangakun nan Bapan Namalyari ya bibiyan lan Jacob, layangên sila êt. Buy ya dinyag nan Jose, insaliw na sa mani magkasakit sa Egipto ya tagan ta amên silan dilag maêkan.

{{:en:obs:obs-08-11.jpg?nolink&640x360}}

So Jacob sent his sons to Egypt to buy food. When the brothers stood before Joseph to buy food, they did not recognize him. But Joseph recognized them.

Buy sa nabalitan lan Jacob ya idi sa Canaan, ya malakê ya maêkan baydu sa Egipto, in-utus na silay anak na ya manaliw kan Jose. Kabay naku silay mani mapatêl nan Jose sa bansan Egipto. Sa pamakalatêng silay na baydu, a la ya tanda ya si Jose awêd ya mamaala sa bansan Egipto. Nuwa, si Jose, tanda na ya sabay silay mani mapatêl na ya naisaliw kana.

{{:en:obs:obs-08-12.jpg?nolink&640x360}}

After testing them to see if they had changed, Joseph said to them, “I am your brother, Joseph! Do not be afraid. You tried to do evil when you sold me as a slave, but God used the evil for good! Come and live in Egypt so I can provide for you and your families.”

Si Jose, sinubukan na silay mani mapatêl na, nu pêtêg ga nagbayu ya ugali la. Sa natandan na ya nagbayu silay na, nagpatanda si Jose kalla. Buy sinagyat na silay na ya manugêl sa Egipto. Ta sawpan na sila.

{{:en:obs:obs-08-13.jpg?nolink&640x360}}

When the brothers returned home and told Jacob that Joseph was still alive, he was very happy.

Sên nuli mani patêl na, inbalita la sa mani bapa la, ya sinabi la, “Bapay, nabyay ya pun awêd si Jose. Pansagyatên na kitamun manugêl baydu sa Egipto ta amên na kitamu kanu masawpan.” Sa natandan nan Jacob ya nabyay ya pun si Jose, natula yan sadya.

{{:en:obs:obs-08-14.jpg?nolink&640x360}}

Even though Jacob was an old man, he moved to Egypt with all of his family, and they all lived there. Before Jacob died, he blessed each of his sons.

Agyan matuwa yinay Jacob, gawan sa tula na, naku ya sa Egipto, kaawyun na silay pamilya na. Buy nanugêl silay na baydu. Nuwa bayu yan nati si Jacob, pinagpala na sila pun ya kaganawan ya anak na.

{{:en:obs:obs-08-15.jpg?nolink&640x360}}

The covenant promises that God gave to Abraham were passed on to Isaac, then to Jacob, and then to Jacob’s twelve sons and their families. The descendants of the twelve sons became the twelve tribes of Israel.

Amêsên, ya mani pangaku nan Bapan Namalyari kan Abraham, naku kan Isaac. Buy sên nati si Isaac, naku êt kan Jacob. Buy sên nati si Jacob, naku êt sa labinluway anak na buy mani pamilya la. Ati ya labinluway anak nan Jacob ya nagin labinluway kalahin tribu ya pamêgên Israel. Ta mani anak na silan Jacob ya pamêgên êt Israel.
