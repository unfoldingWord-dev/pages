====== 10. The Ten Plagues ======

{{:en:obs:obs-10-01.jpg?nolink&640x360}}

Moses and Aaron went to Pharaoh. They said, “This is what the God of Israel says, ‘Let my people go!’” Pharaoh laughed at them. Instead of letting the Israelites go free, he made their work even harder!

Si Moises buy Aarun, naku silan makisabi kan Puun Paraun. Sinabi la kana, “Panggalangên ya Puun Paraun, kaylangan ya payagên mu silan mita ya Israelita. Ta ati ya kalabayan nan Bapan Namalyari.” Nuwa kay kinumali ya  Puun Paraun, ya sinabi na, “A ku kalabay ta a ku ya tanda si Bapan Namalyari yu!” A na sila pinayagan, nuwa luyang na silan pasakitan.

{{:en:obs:obs-10-02.jpg?nolink&640x360}}

Pharaoh kept refusing to let the people go, so God sent ten plagues on Egypt. (A plague is a terrible disaster.) Through these plagues, God showed Pharaoh that He is more powerful than Pharaoh and all of Egypt’s gods.

Si Puun Paraun, pisasabi na ya, “A ku sila payagên ya mita!” Buy ya dinyag nan Bapan Namalyari, pinalatêng nay mapu 10 ya klasin kasakit ya mamasakit sa mani taga-Egipto. Buy sinabi na, “Labay kun ipakit ya igit pun ya kapangyarian ku kaysa sa kapangyarian nan Puun Paraun buy kapangyarian un diyusdiyusan na.” 

{{:en:obs:obs-10-03.jpg?nolink&640x360}}

God turned the Nile River into blood, but Pharaoh still would not let the Israelites go.

Pinadyag nan daya un Bapan Namalyari ya lanêm ya pamêgên ilug Nilo. Sên nakatanam silan kasakit, naku yina man ya Moises kan Puun Paraun. Yawarên nayna man ya iyawasan na silay Israelita. Nuwa, sinabi nan un Puun Paraun, “A ku silan payagên mita!” 

{{:en:obs:obs-10-04.jpg?nolink&640x360}}

God sent frogs all over Egypt. Pharaoh begged Moses to take away the frogs. But after all the frogs died, Pharaoh hardened his heart and would not let the Israelites go.

Ya dinyag nan Bapan Namalyari sa ikaluwan klasin kasakit, inpaawas na yay malakêy pasinga.

{{:en:obs:obs-10-05.jpg?nolink&640x360}}

So God sent a plague of gnats and then a plague of flies. Pharaoh called Moses and Aaron and told them they could leave if they stopped the plague. Moses prayed, and God removed all the flies from Egypt, but Pharaoh hardened his heart and would not let the people go.

Pamakayari, sên ikatlun klasin kasakit, impaawas na uman un balikatu. Buy sa ikaapat ya klasin kasakit, impaawas yina man un mani langaw. Balang gisay kasakit, pan-ipangaku nan Puun Paraun, ya sinabi na, “Nu itas nan Bapan Namalyari ati ya kasakit, payagên ku silay nan mita si Israelita.” Apat yinan besis ya pan-itas nan Bapan Namalyari un kasakit. Nuwa balang itas nan Bapan Namalyari ya kasakit, nakdêy ya pun murin sa bêkê nan Puun Paraun. Ta pisasabi na, “A kina payagên ya mita silay Israelita.” Kabay, si Bapan Namalyari, pasakitan na silay nan uman ya Egiptio. Amêsên, silay taga Egipton kay ya nadanan un kasakit. Nuwa sa mani Israelita, ayin naku kalla.

{{:en:obs:obs-10-06.jpg?nolink&640x360}}

Next God sent a sickness to kill all the farm animals that belonged to the Egyptians. Pharaoh’s heart was hardened, and he would not let the Israelites go.

Ya limay klasin kasakit, inpaawas nan Bapan Namalyari ya sakit ya makamati sa kaganawan na mani ayup lan taga Egipto.

{{:en:obs:obs-10-07.jpg?nolink&640x360}}

Then God told Moses to throw ashes into the air in front of Pharaoh. When he did, painful skin sores appeared on the Egyptians, but not on the Israelites. God hardened Pharaoh's heart, and Pharaoh would not let the Israelites go free.

Pangayari, ya ikaanêm ma klasin kasakit, in-utus nan Bapan Namalyari ya ipaawas ya mani lusu ya matanam lan taga Egipto.

{{:en:obs:obs-10-08.jpg?nolink&640x360}}

After that, God sent hail that destroyed the crops in Egypt and killed anybody who went outside. Pharaoh called Moses and Aaron and told them, “I have sinned. You may go.” So Moses prayed, and the hail stopped.

Ya ikapitu ya klasin kasakit, namauran yan elu si Bapan Namalyari. Buy abituy elu, ingkamati lan kaganawan ya Taga-egipto ya idi sa awas un bali la. Buy nakasira pun un mani tanêm lan Taga-egipto. Nuwa sa lugal ya bibiyan lan Israelita, ayin nati kalla buy ayin nati sa mani tanêm la. 

{{:en:obs:obs-10-09.jpg?nolink&640x360}}

But Pharaoh sinned again and hardened his heart and would not let the Israelites go.

Agyan parabaydu yinay nalyari ya kasakit,a ya murin manlêngê kan Bapan Namalyari, nuwa luyang yinan makdêy un bêkê na. Ta a na pinyagan Mita ya Israelita.

{{:en:obs:obs-10-10.jpg?nolink&640x360}}

So God caused swarms of locusts to come over Egypt. These locusts ate what remained of the crops that the hail had not destroyed.

Buy si ikawaluy klasin kasakit, impaawas nay malakê ya mani libangaw. Buy ya natagan ya a nasiran un elu, kinnan êt un mani libangaw.

{{:en:obs:obs-10-11.jpg?nolink&640x360}}

Then God sent darkness that lasted for three days. It was so dark that the Egyptians could not leave their houses. But where the Israelites lived, there was light.

Pangayari, ya ikasyam ma klasin kasakit, sa lalên un tatlu allu, pinadêglêm nan Bapan Namalyari ya lugal la bibiyan lan taga-Egipto. Buy a silay na makaawas sa bali la. Nuwa, sawang naman ya bibiyan lan Israelita.

{{:en:obs:obs-10-12.jpg?nolink&640x360}}

Even after these nine plagues, Pharaoh still refused to let the Israelites go free. Since Pharaoh would not listen, there was one last plague that God planned to send. This would change Pharaoh’s mind.

Agyan parabaydu yinay nalyari, sinabi nayna man kan Moises, “A ku sila payagên ya mita.” Nuwa sa katatatawliyan, dilag pun gisay klasin kasakit. Agyan a ya mangilala ya Puun Paraun sa unan dinyag nan Bapan Namalyari, sa ikamapuy daygên nan kasakit, mitas yina ya kakdêyan un bêkê nan Puun Paraun.

//A Bible story from: Exodus 5-10//
