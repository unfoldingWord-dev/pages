====== 10. The Ten Plagues ======


{{:en:obs:obs-10-01.jpg?nolink&640x360}}


Moses and Aaron went to Pharaoh. They said, “This is what the God of Israel says, ‘Let my people go!’” Pharaoh laughed at them. Instead of letting the Israelites go free, he made their work even harder!


Si Moises buy  si Aarun, naku silan makisabi kan Puun Paraun. Sinabi la kana, “Panggalangên  yan na Puun Paraun, kaylangan pasawan mun  mita ya Israelita. Ta ati ya kalabayan Bapan Namalyari.” Nuwa kay nangkayli ya  Puun Paraun, ya sinabi na, “A ku kalabay ta a ku ya tanday  abiin na pansabin yuy  Bapa yun  Namalyari yu!” A na sila pinasawan nun a luyang na silan pinasakit.


{{:en:obs:obs-10-02.jpg?nolink&640x360}}


Pharaoh kept refusing to let the people go, so God sent ten plagues on Egypt. (A plague is a terrible disaster.) Through these plagues, God showed Pharaoh that He is more powerful than Pharaoh and all of Egypt’s gods.


Si Puun Paraun, sinabi nay , “A ku sila pasawan na  mita!” Buy ya dinyag Bapan Namalyari, pinalatêng nay mapuy kalasin kasakitan ya mamasakit sa mani taga-Egipto. Buy sinabi na, “Labay kun ipakit ya igit pun ya kapangyarian ku kaysa sa kapangyarian nan Puun Paraun buy kapangyarian un diyusdiyusan na.”


{{:en:obs:obs-10-03.jpg?nolink&640x360}}


God turned the Nile River into blood, but Pharaoh still would not let the Israelites go.


Pinadyag nan daya un Bapan Namalyari ya lanêm ya pamêgên ilug Nilo. Sên nakatanam silan kasakitan, naku si  Moises kan Puun Paraun. Yawarên nay pasawan nan mita ya mani Israelita. Nuwa, sinabin Puun Paraun, “A ku sila pasawan mita!”


{{:en:obs:obs-10-04.jpg?nolink&640x360}}


God sent frogs all over Egypt. Pharaoh begged Moses to take away the frogs. But after all the frogs died, Pharaoh hardened his heart and would not let the Israelites go.


Ya dinyag  Bapan Namalyari sa ikaluwan kalasin kasakitan, inpaawas nay malakêy kasumparag.


{{:en:obs:obs-10-05.jpg?nolink&640x360}}


So God sent a plague of gnats and then a plague of flies. Pharaoh called Moses and Aaron and told them they could leave if they stopped the plague. Moses prayed, and God removed all the flies from Egypt, but Pharaoh hardened his heart and would not let the people go.


Pamakayari, sên ikatlun kalasin kasakitan, namipaawas yan manguman balikatu. Buy sa ikaapat ta kalasin kasakitan, namipaawas yan  mani langaw. Balang gisay kasakitan, pan-ipangaku nan Puun Paraun, ya sinabi na, “Nu itas  Bapan Namalyari  ya ata  kasakitan, pasawan ku silay  nan  mita ya mani Israelita.”Kati apat  tan pan-itas  Bapan Namalyari  ya kasakitan. Nuwa balang itas  nay kasakitan, nakdêy  pun et  murin ya  bêkên Puun Paraun. Ta pansabin  na, “A kina pasawan  mitay mani  Israelita.” Kabay,  pampasakitan Bapan Namalyarin manguman na  mani taga Egiptu. Amêsên,  kay mani taga Egiptu ya nakadanas un kasakitan. Nuwa  ya mani Israelita, ayin naku kalla.


{{:en:obs:obs-10-06.jpg?nolink&640x360}}


Next God sent a sickness to kill all the farm animals that belonged to the Egyptians. Pharaoh’s heart was hardened, and he would not let the Israelites go.


Ya ikalimay kalasin kasakitan, inpaawas  Bapan Namalyari ya sakit ya mamakamati sa kaganawan  ayup lan taga Egiptu.


{{:en:obs:obs-10-07.jpg?nolink&640x360}}


Then God told Moses to throw ashes into the air in front of Pharaoh. When he did, painful skin sores appeared on the Egyptians, but not on the Israelites. God hardened Pharaoh's heart, and Pharaoh would not let the Israelites go free.


Pamakayari, ya ikaanêm ma kalasin kasakitan, in-utus  Bapan Namalyari ya umawas sa  mani lusu sa lawini  lan taga Egiptu.


{{:en:obs:obs-10-08.jpg?nolink&640x360}}


After that, God sent hail that destroyed the crops in Egypt and killed anybody who went outside. Pharaoh called Moses and Aaron and told them, “I have sinned. You may go.” So Moses prayed, and the hail stopped.


Ya ikapituy kalasin kasakit, namauran  elu si Bapan Namalyari. Buy abituy elu, ingkamati lan kaganawan Taga-egipto ya idi sa awas un bali la. Buy nakasira pun un mani tanêm lan Taga-egipto. Nuwa sa lugal la bibiyan mani Israelita, ayin nati kalla buy ayin nati sa mani gitna  la.


{{:en:obs:obs-10-09.jpg?nolink&640x360}}


But Pharaoh sinned again and hardened his heart and would not let the Israelites go.


Agyan parabayduy nalyari ya kasakit,a  murin manlêngê ya puun Paraun kan Bapan Namalyari, nun a luyang yapun  makdêy ya  bêkê na. Ta a na pinsawan  Mita ya Israelita.


{{:en:obs:obs-10-10.jpg?nolink&640x360}}


So God caused swarms of locusts to come over Egypt. These locusts ate what remained of the crops that the hail had not destroyed.


Buy si ikawaluy klasin kasakit, impaawas nay malakêy mani libangew. Buy ya nitagan na a nasiran  elu, kinnan êt un mani libangew.


{{:en:obs:obs-10-11.jpg?nolink&640x360}}


Then God sent darkness that lasted for three days. It was so dark that the Egyptians could not leave their houses. But where the Israelites lived, there was light.


Pamakayari, ya ikasyam ma klasin kasakitan, sa lalên un tatluy allu, pinadêglêm  Bapan Namalyari ya lugal la bibiyan lan taga-Egipto. Buy a silay na makaawas sa bali la. Nuwa, nasawang nga man na bibiyan lan Israelita.


{{:en:obs:obs-10-12.jpg?nolink&640x360}}


Even after these nine plagues, Pharaoh still refused to let the Israelites go free. Since Pharaoh would not listen, there was one last plague that God planned to send. This would change Pharaoh’s mind.


Agyan parabayduy nalyari, sinabi nayna man kan Moises, “A ku sila pasawan  ya mita.” Nuwa sa katatatawliyan, dilag pun gisay kalasin kasakitan. Agyan a ya mangilala ya Puun Paraun sa nunan dinyag  Bapan Namalyari, sa ikamapuy daygên nay kasakit, mitas say  kakdêyan un bêkên Puun Paraun.


//A Bible story from: Exodus 5-10//
