====== ** 13. Kinabitan lan mamita sa kakyangan ======


{{:en:obs:obs-14-01.jpg?nolink&640x360}}


After the Israelites received God’s Covenant at Mount Sinai, God began leading them toward the Promised Land, which was also called Canaan. The cloud pillar showed them the way.


Pagkayari un pamisabin Bapan Namalyari buy mani Israelita baydu sa Bung-uy un Sinai, nag-umpisaynan namitay mani Israelita papakun sa lugal la impangaku na kalla. Idi sa ginêm si Bapan Namalyari buy saba siyay mangawyun kalla.

BT: After the mutual-talking-together of God and the Israelites there at the Mountain of Sinai, the Israelites started to go to the place he promised to-them. God was present in the cloud and he was the one to lead them.


{{:en:obs:obs-14-02.jpg?nolink&640x360}}


God had promised Abraham, Isaac, and Jacob that He would give the Promised Land to their descendants, but now there were many tribes of people living there. They were called Canaanites. The Canaanites did not believe in God. They worshiped false gods and did many evil things.


Impangakun Bapan Namalyari kan Abraham, Isaac, buy Jacub ba byan nan lugal la mani layi la. Nuwa amêsen, abaytuy lugal la impangakun Bapan Namalyari kalla, malakêy tribu ya manugêl baydu. Ya ati silay tribu, pambêgên silan Cananeo. Si ati silay Cananeo, a sila nanampalataya kan Bapan Namalyari. Ta ya pinaniwalan layna man, diusdiusan la.

BT:


{{:en:obs:obs-14-03.jpg?nolink&640x360}}


God told the Israelites, “You must get rid of all the Canaanites in the Promised Land. Do not make peace with them and do not marry them. You must completely destroy all of their idols. If you do not do these things, you will worship their idols instead of Me.”


Sinabin Bapan Namalyari sa mani Israelita, <<Itabi yuy kaganawan na taga Canaan ya saba lugal la impangaku ku kamuyu. Agana kaw makiallu-allu kalla buy agana kaw êt mangwan babayi la. Kaylangan yapungên yuy kaganawan na diusdiusan la. Nu a yu sunulên ata utus ku, dat diusdiusan laynay sambên yu, alwaynan siku.>>

BT:

{{:en:obs:obs-14-04.jpg?nolink&640x360}}


When the Israelites reached the edge of Canaan, Moses chose twelve men, one from each tribe of Israel. He gave the men instructions to go and spy on the land to see what it was like. They were also to spy on the Canaanites to see if they were strong or weak.


Amesen, sên narani silay na sa lugal la impangakun Bapan Namalyari kalla, si dinyag Moises, namili yan labinluway liyaki ya tigisa sa balang tribun Israel. Sinabi na kalla, <<Lihim kaw maku sa lugal un Canaan. Ta êlêwên yu nu napas-êy sila o asê.>>


{{:en:obs:obs-14-05.jpg?nolink&640x360}}


The men spied on Canaan for forty days, then they came back. Ten of the spies gave a bad report, saying, “The land is very good, and the crops are great! But the cities are very strong, and the people are giants! If we attack them, we will surely die!”


Sa lalê un apatapuy allu, nangêlêw sila baydu un ayin magtanda. Sên namaurung sila, ya mapu kalla, imbalita lay ati, <<Ya lugal la ubatan yan, malakêy maêkan baydu. Nuwa mapas-êy ya mani tawu baydu buy para kitamun uybun balikatu kalla. Ta ya mani tawu baydu mangatatagay buy nikatitibuy lawini. Nu duyungên tamu sila, awu ata kuwenta tamu.>>


{{:en:obs:obs-14-06.jpg?nolink&640x360}}


Immediately Caleb and Joshua, the other two spies, said, “It is true that the people of Canaan are tall and strong, but we can certainly defeat them! God will fight for us!”


Nuwa si Caleb buy Josue, tambêng lan sinabi sa mani kaawyun la, <<Peteg ga silay mani tawu baydu, napas-êy buy mangatatagay. Nuwa pilmin sambuten tamu sila! Ta awyunan kitamun Bapan Namalyari.>>


{{:en:obs:obs-14-07.jpg?nolink&640x360}}


But the people were mad at Moses and Aaron and said, “Oh, why did you bring us to this horrible place? We should have stayed in Egypt rather than be killed in battle and our wives and children made slaves.” The people wanted to kill them and choose a new leader to bring them back to Egypt.


Nuwa nagreklamuy kaganawan na Israelita, <<Mas mangêd dayin nati kitamuyna baydu sa Egiptu o kaya baydi sa kakyangan kaysa mati kitamu sa labanan! Ta silay kaaway tamu, daygên lay mani ipus ya mani asawa buy anak tamu.>> Nuwa si Moises kaawyun na silan Aaron, Josue, buy Caleb, nakilunus sila kallan Israelita ya a la subukan ya Bapan Namalyari. Nuwa silay Israelita, labay la ya mamili silan bayun puun buy mag-udung sa Egiptu.


{{:en:obs:obs-14-08.jpg?nolink&640x360}}


God was very angry, and His presence appeared at the Tent of Meeting. God said, “Except for Joshua and Caleb, all of the people who are twenty years old or older will wander in the wilderness until they die. They will never enter the Promised Land.”


Nasuluk si Bapan Namalyari. Amêsên, agad lan nakit ya sawang Bapan Namalyari sa Tuldan Pagtipunan la. Sên nakit lay sawang, sinabin Bapan Namalyari kan Moises, <<Silay tuwa paubat sa naabut ya luwampu ya taun, a sila makasun sa lugal la impangaku ku. Magbalang-balang sila sa kakyangan angggan mati silayna. Kay si Josue buy Caleb ya makasun sa lugal la impangaku ku.>>


{{:en:obs:obs-14-09.jpg?nolink&640x360}}


When the people heard this, they were sorry they had sinned. They took their weapons and went to attack Canaan. Moses warned them not to go because God was not with them, but they did not listen to him.


Sên nalêngê lan Israelita ati ya sinabi nan Bapan Namalyari, nilungkut sila. Nuwa nangasalanan silan uman, ta kinwa lay almas la, buy dinawohong la silay Caneneo, agyan a na sinabin Bapan Namalyari. Nuwa si Moises, imbawal na sila ya sinabi na kalla, “Gana kaw naku.” Nuwa ayin nallêngê kana.


{{:en:obs:obs-14-10.jpg?nolink&640x360}}


God did not go with them into this battle, so they were defeated and many of them were killed. Then the rest of the Israelites turned back from Canaan and wandered through the wilderness for forty years.


Buy si Bapan Namalyari, gana ya nakilaku sa mani Israelita ya maku makipati sa mani Cananeo. Kabay nasambut sila buy malakê ya nati kalla, ta a na sila niawyunan un Bapan Namalyari. Buy silay nagkutet sa laban, silabay kay ya nitagan. Nuwa nagbalang-balang silay na sa kakyangan sa lalên un apatapu ya tawun.


{{:en:obs:obs-14-11.jpg?nolink&640x360}}


During that time of wandering, God provided for them. He gave them bread from heaven, called “manna.” He also sent quail (a kind of bird) into their camp so they could have meat to eat. During those forty years, God kept their clothes and shoes from wearing out.


Sa pagbalang-balang lan Israelitan nitagan, si Bapan Namalyari, kinalingun na sila êt êt. Allu-allu, pambyan na silan tinapay ya ubat sa langit ya pamêgên “manna”. Buy pambyan na sila êt un abêng ta amên sila makakêna. Buy sa lalên un apatapu ya tawun, a binumata ya sapatus la buy yaming la, gawan sa pangalingun nan Bapan Namalyari kalla.


{{:en:obs:obs-14-12.jpg?nolink&640x360}}


God even miraculously gave them water from a rock. But despite all this, the people of Israel complained and grumbled against God and against Moses. God was still faithful to His promises to Abraham, Isaac, and Jacob.


Si Bapan Namalyari, binyanan na silan lanem ya mani Israelita ya ubat sa dapas. Nuwa silay Israelita, nagdeklamu sila kan Bapan Namalyari buy Moises. Nuwa mapagkatiwalan murin si Bapan Namalyari sa mani pangaku na kan Abraham, Isaac, buy Isaac.


{{:en:obs:obs-14-13.jpg?nolink&640x360}}


One time, the people had no water. God told Moses, “Speak to the rock, and water will come out of it.” But Moses dishonored God in front of all the people by hitting the rock twice with a stick instead of speaking to it. Water came out of the rock for everyone to drink, but God was angry with Moses and said, “You will not enter the Promised Land.”


Gisay allu, ayin silan lanêm. Sinabin Bapan Namalyari kan Moses, <<Sabian muy batu buy umawas ya lanêm.>> Nuwa a na sinunul ya sinabin Bapan Namalyari kana. Ta a na sinabian ya batu nun a dinugê-dugê nay batu un luwa besis. Inumawas ya lanêm ta amên makainêm ma kaganawan na tawu, nuwa nasuluk si Bapan Namalyari kan Moses, sinabi na, <<A kayna sumun sa lugal la impangku ku kamuyu.>>


{{:en:obs:obs-14-14.jpg?nolink&640x360}}


After they had wandered in the wilderness for forty years, all the people who had rebelled against God were dead. God led the Israelites to the edge of the Promised Land again. Moses was now very old, so he chose Joshua to help him lead the people. God promised Moses that one day He would raise up another prophet like Moses.


Pamakayari un pagbalang-balang la sa kakyangan ya lalên un apatapu ya tawun, silay kaganawan ya nagtawun un luwampu’ patag-ay ubat sa unan pamaku la sa Canaan, nati silan kaganawan. Amêsên, inlaku na silay na man un Bapan Namalyari sa katganan un lugal ya inpangakun na kalla. Buy sa pamakalatêng la sa katganan un lugal ya inpangakun nan Bapan Namalyari kalla, tuwa yina sadya si Moses.


{{:en:obs:obs-14-15.jpg?nolink&640x360}}


God brought Moses to the top of a mountain so he could see the Promised Land. Moses saw the Promised Land but never entered it. Then Moses died, and the Israelites mourned for thirty days. Joshua became their new leader. Joshua was a good leader because he trusted and obeyed God.


Sên idi silay na sa katganan un lugal ya inpangakun nan Bapan Namalyari kalla, gintan na si Moses sa babun un bung-uy ta amên na matamulaw ya lugal ya inpangaku na. Pamakayari, nati yinay Moises, buy si Josue ya kasagili na. Si Josue, mangêd yan mamunu ta manunul ya kan Bapan Namalyari.
