======= 40. Impako la yay Apo Jesus ha kodoh =======

{{:en:obs:obs-40-01.jpg?nolink&640x360}}

Then they led Jesus away to be crucified, making him carry his own cross.

Haanin, hiyay Apo Jesus, ampamatay yan kodoh legan ang-ilakew la ya ha ilwah nin banwa. (John 19:17)

{{:en:obs:obs-40-02.jpg?nolink&640x360}}

They came to a place called “The Skull” and nailed his arms and feet to the cross. But Jesus said, “Father, forgive them, because they do not know what they are doing.” Pilate commanded that a sign be put above Jesus’ head that read, “King of the Jews.”

\v 33 Ha niabot hilayna ihtew ha logal a anhabtan Bongoꞌ, impako la ya ha kodoh ye Apo Jesus.  \v 34 Haanin, naghabi yay Apo Jesus, a wana, “Bapa ko, patawaden mo hilay ampanyag nin wanabay kangko. Ta ahe la tanda ye andiyagen la.” Main nakaholat ihtewbay ha dapit ōwan nin kodoh nan Apo Jesus, a wana, “YABAYTI YE POON LAN JUJUDIO.” (Luk 23:33a,34, 38)


{{:en:obs:obs-40-03.jpg?nolink&640x360}}

The soldiers gambled for Jesus’ clothing. When they did this, they fulfilled a prophecy which said, “They divided my garments among them, and gambled for my clothing.”

Ket hilay hohondaloh, kingwa lay babado nan Apo Jesus. Ta nanyag hilan pandaw-an lan ngalan nin makakwa nin babado na. Nalyadi yati omon ya magkapeteg ye impaholat nan Apo Dioh, a wana, “Pinay-atagan lay tapih ko boy pinaybonotan la yay kepkep kon makadang.” (Luk 23:34, John 19:24) 


{{:en:obs:obs-40-04.jpg?nolink&640x360}}

Jesus was crucified between two robbers. One of them mocked Jesus, but the other said, “Don’t you fear God? We are guilty, but this man is innocent. Jesus, please remember me in your Kingdom.” Jesus said to him, “Today, you will be with me in Paradise.”

Haanin, main po lowa kataon mangaloke a inlakew la ihtew a pateyen la a lamo nan Apo Jesus. 
Hiyay maghay tolihan a nakapako ha kodoh, inomih na ya met ye Apo Jesus, a wana, “Aliwa nayi a hika ye Cristo a impangako nan Apo Dioh? Higi awod! Iligtah moy hadili mo boy iligtah mo kayi met!” 
Noba hinaad na yan maghay tolihan, a wana, “Ahe mo anhabiyen yain. Ahe ka nayi angkalimo kanan Apo Dioh? Padipadiho kitawobaynan angkapadohaan. Hikita ye katapolan lan pateyen oli ha dinyag tan kalok-an. Noba yatin tao, homain yan bega dinyag a maloke.” 
Haanin, hinabi na kanan Apo Jesus, a wana, “Apo Jesus, no anti kawoyna lano ha panakopan mo, adi mo ko anliwaan.” 
Nakibat yay Apo Jesus kana, a wana, “Adi ka angkahindak. Haanin a met ateed, ilamo katayna ha pinakamanged a logal.” (Luk 23:32,39-43)

{{:en:obs:obs-40-05.jpg?nolink&640x360}}

The crowd and the Jewish leaders mocked Jesus, saying, “If you are the Son of God, come down from the cross and save yourself! Then we will believe you.”

Malabong hilay tataon anti ihtew a ampamoya. Hilay mānongkolan a anti ihtew, an-omihen la yay Apo Jesus, a wanla, “Hali man awod! Ta nangiligtah ya kano nin kanayon a tatao. Ket haanin, no peteg a hiyabay ye Cristo a impangako nan Apo Dioh, ket iligtah na met awod ye hadili na haanin.” (Luk 23:35)


{{:en:obs:obs-40-06.jpg?nolink&640x360}}

It was the middle of the day, but darkness came over the whole land. It stayed dark from noon until 3:00 in the afternoon.

Ha ogtoynay mangaamot, nilomiteh ye matibowen babe lota anggayna ha mabling nin mahilem. (Luk 23:44)


{{:en:obs:obs-40-07.jpg?nolink&640x360}}

Then Jesus cried out, “It is finished! Father, I give my spirit into your hands.” Then he bowed his head and gave up his spirit. When he died, there was an earthquake and the large curtain in the Temple that separated the presence of God from the people was torn in two, from the top to the bottom.

Hiyay Apo Jesus, impangha nan mak-haw, a wana, “Bapa ko, an-ipataya koynay kalelewa ko kammo.” Pamigantah naynan hinabi yati, naboytoan yaynan angeh. Haanin, hiyay mangaamot, naalihan yan henag boy hiyay makogpan tapih a pinagpahanip ha loob nin Panggalangan kanan Apo Dioh, napigitna yan naginit.  (Luk 23:46,45)

{{:en:obs:obs-40-08.jpg?nolink&640x360}}

Through his death, Jesus opened a way for people to come to God. When the soldier guarding Jesus saw everything that happened, he said, “Certainly, this man was innocent. He must have been the Son of God.”

Hiyay ampamaala kanlan hohondaloh, ha nakit na yatew a nalyadi kanan Apo Jesus, dinangal na yay Apo Dioh, a wana, “Peteg a matoynong ya yatin tao.” (Luk 23:47)

{{:en:obs:obs-40-09.jpg?nolink&640x360}}

Then Joseph and Nicodemus, two Jewish leaders who believed Jesus was the Messiah, asked Pilate for Jesus’ body. They wrapped his body in cloth and buried it in a tomb cut out of rock. Then they rolled a large stone over the opening.

Haanin, main maghay lāki a nag-ngalan Jose a taga Arimatea a banwa lan Jujudio. Manged yan tao boy matoynong ya. Agya magha ya kanlan mangatoan mānongkolan, ahe ya nakitopig kanlan namatey kanan Apo Jesus. Ket magha ya met kanlan tataon ampangagad ha panaon a manakop yaynay Apo Dioh. Haanin, nakew ya kanan Pilato, ta naghabi ya, a wana, “Gobirnador, ampakiingalo ako kammo. Ta kowen ko ya dayi ye bangkay nan Apo Jesus.” Ket kananyain, pinalobohan na ya met. Ket haanin, in-aypa na yan Jose ye bangkay nan Apo Jesus. Ta pinotot na yan tapih a mapoti a anhabtan lino haka na ya inloob ha yokib a ahe po bega nangilbengan. (Luk 23:50-53)
